## Marietta Square: A Historic Town Center That Draws Crowds

Marietta Square is the kind of place metro Atlanta doesn't have many of — a genuine historic courthouse square, ringed by restaurants, theaters, galleries, and shops, with a green park at its center and Civil War history layered through the surrounding streets. It pulls visitors from across the region who want a walkable, characterful weekend, and it stays busy with a festival and events calendar that gives people specific reasons to come on specific dates. For short-term rental owners, a home near the Square is positioned in one of Cobb County's most appealing leisure destinations.

### Why Guests Come

The leisure draw starts with the Square itself: the restaurants, the Marietta Square Market food hall, the Strand Theatre, and the galleries are all walkable from one another, and Glover Park anchors the middle. The events calendar does heavy lifting — festivals, concerts, and art walks turn ordinary weekends into bookable demand spikes. Heritage tourism is a second pillar; Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park and the area's Civil War sites and museums draw history travelers who want a walkable base nearby. And because the Square sits within easy reach of The Battery, Truist Park, and the Cobb Galleria, it also catches spillover from Braves game days and the Galleria's event and corporate traffic.

### Who Books Here

Expect a leisure-dominant mix with real variety. Couples and friend groups come for dinner, a show at the Strand, and a festival weekend. Heritage travelers come for Kennesaw Mountain and the museums. Families and groups book around Truist Park games and Cobb events, wanting space close to the action. The common thread is that these guests value walkability and character — they are choosing the Square specifically, not a generic suburban location. That makes a home's proximity to the Square and its overall charm the central selling points.

The Square's combination of a busy events calendar, heritage tourism, and nearby sports and Galleria demand gives owners a fuller, more varied booking picture than a flat suburban market.

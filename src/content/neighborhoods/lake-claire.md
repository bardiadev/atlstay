## Lake Claire: Atlanta's Quietest, Most Intentional Neighborhood

Lake Claire is easy to overlook on a map — it sits between the more-recognizable names of Candler Park and Little Five Points, in a pocket that the major traffic corridors pass by rather than through. That obscurity is a feature. Guests who discover Lake Claire often say it's the most genuinely Atlanta thing they've done: a real neighborhood, real neighbors, real residential life that happens to have world-class amenities a ten-minute walk away.

The communal identity is unusual. Lake Claire has one of Atlanta's most active community land trust traditions and a neighborhood culture that shows up in visible ways: street-level art, community gardens, cooperative infrastructure. Guests feel the difference immediately. That character creates a type of property experience — staying in a neighborhood that functions as a community — that no hotel can replicate and that only a well-positioned STR can offer.

The location math is strong. From Lake Claire, guests can walk to Candler Park's green space and golf course in one direction and to Little Five Points' Variety Playhouse, independent shops, and dining in another. The Beltline Eastside Trail is within reach. Freedom Park connects the neighborhood to Inman Park and further into Atlanta. For a quiet, tree-lined enclave, Lake Claire has exceptional connectivity.

For property owners, the guest profile is one of Lake Claire's advantages. Travelers who seek out this neighborhood tend toward longer stays, lower maintenance needs, and the kind of considered engagement that leads to five-star reviews. They're not looking for the closest hotel substitute — they're looking for the antidote to it.

ATLStay understands how to market Lake Claire to the guests who will love it most. That means positioning the neighborhood's character accurately, attracting the right guest profile, and managing the property to the standard that protects your reviews and your rates.

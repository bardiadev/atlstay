## Five Points: Athens's Walkable Neighborhood Near Campus

Five Points is the kind of neighborhood that gives a college town its character beyond the campus itself. Centered on a small, walkable commercial village — coffee shops, restaurants, local retail — and ringed by tree-lined residential streets, it sits just south of the University of Georgia and trades downtown's bar-scene energy for something more settled. For short-term rental owners, that profile is a genuine advantage: Five Points is close enough to Sanford Stadium to ride the football-weekend wave, but residential enough to be the neighborhood that visiting families actively prefer.

### The Game-Day and Family Combination

What makes Five Points work is that it serves two of the best guest types in the Athens market without forcing a choice between them. On football Saturdays, its proximity to campus and the stadium makes it desirable, walkable-to-tailgate inventory — and tailgating is so central to the Georgia game-day experience that guests place a real premium on homes within reach of the gates. At the same time, the neighborhood's quiet streets, yards, and family-friendly feel make it the natural choice for the multi-generational groups that a major university generates year-round. A property here doesn't have to live or die by a single segment.

### Built for Families and Groups

The housing stock in and around Five Points skews toward the single-family homes that family travelers want: multiple bedrooms, comfortable gathering spaces, a kitchen for the post-ceremony dinner, parking, and a yard. That matters most around UGA's structured calendar peaks. Graduation in May is the second-largest demand event of the Athens year, drawing families from across the state who want the space and privacy of a rented home over a block of hotel rooms. Parents Weekend in the fall, move-in periods, and campus events add more of the same. Homes with three to five bedrooms and good gathering space are especially well-suited to capture this demand, and Five Points has them.

### The Walkable Village Edge

Five Points has one more thing many residential areas lack: a real center. The village node at its heart — cafés, restaurants, shops — gives guests a walkable destination without leaving the neighborhood, which is exactly the kind of detail that elevates a listing for families and couples who'd rather not drive everywhere. Being able to walk to morning coffee and an easy dinner, in a quiet neighborhood close to campus, is a stronger pitch than a remote rental or a downtown unit over a bar. It also tends to produce the kind of reviews that compound over time — guests who mention how easy, walkable, and family-friendly the stay was. ATLStay leans on that in every Five Points listing.

## Castleberry Hill: Atlanta's Arts District Meets Its Biggest Event Venue

The juxtaposition that defines Castleberry Hill is real and unusual: a neighborhood of converted warehouses, working galleries, and loft residences sits immediately adjacent to one of the country's largest multi-purpose stadiums. That combination creates something no neighborhood planning committee would have designed — and it creates exceptional short-term rental demand across two completely different guest types.

The stadium demand is the most obvious driver. Mercedes-Benz Stadium's Falcons games, Atlanta United matches, college football championships, and stadium concerts generate event nights when guests specifically want to walk to the venue rather than navigate parking. Castleberry Hill's walking distance from the stadium is a literal geographical advantage that translates directly to premium pricing on event nights. State Farm Arena, home of the Atlanta Hawks and a year-round concert venue, is equally close.

But Castleberry Hill isn't only an event-adjacent location. The neighborhood has a genuine arts identity. The First Fridays gallery walk has operated for years and draws thousands of visitors monthly who come specifically to experience Atlanta's most concentrated arts district. The neighborhood's galleries, studios, and creative businesses give it an energy that persists between events — and they attract a design-conscious guest profile that books based on the neighborhood's character rather than its proximity to a stadium.

The Georgia World Congress Center is the third demand engine. As one of the country's largest convention facilities, it generates steady midweek professional traffic from exhibitors and attendees who want more space than the adjacent convention-center hotels provide. Multi-night convention stays in a Castleberry Hill loft are a consistently bookable segment that most owners in the neighborhood underestimate.

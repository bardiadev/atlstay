## City Springs: Sandy Springs' Walkable Downtown and Arts Anchor

For years Sandy Springs lacked a true center; City Springs fixed that. The development built a civic and performing-arts core — anchored by the Sandy Springs Performing Arts Center — and wrapped it in a walkable district of restaurants, shops, residences, and a central green. The effect was to give the city a downtown that programs itself with shows, festivals, and seasonal events, while sitting next door to the corporate muscle of the Perimeter area. For short-term rental owners, a home near City Springs is positioned at the intersection of arts-driven leisure demand and steady business travel.

### Why Guests Come

The arts calendar is a genuine demand engine. The Performing Arts Center brings concert and theater audiences who want to be within walking distance of the venue and the surrounding restaurants for a dinner-and-a-show evening. City Springs layers on its own programming — festivals, markets, and outdoor events on the green — that turn weekends into bookable peaks. The walkability is the throughline: guests can park once and reach dinner, a show, and the district's shops on foot, which is exactly what a growing share of travelers look for. And because the Perimeter business district is minutes away, the area also catches weekday corporate spillover from guests who would rather stay in a lively walkable district than at a highway hotel.

### Who Books Here

The guest mix is well balanced. Weekends and event nights skew leisure: theater and concert audiences, couples out for dinner and a show, and festival visitors. Weekdays lean corporate, with Perimeter-area business travelers and longer-stay professionals who value being somewhere walkable. There's also a steady flow of guests using City Springs' central position — GA-400, I-285, and MARTA all close at hand — as a convenient base for Buckhead, Perimeter, and the wider metro. That blend of arts-driven leisure and corporate demand gives the area a fuller calendar than either a pure nightlife or pure office market.

### What Makes It Work, and the Right Setup

City Springs' combination of a programmed arts-and-events calendar and Perimeter corporate spillover is the core advantage — it fills both weekend and weekday demand rather than leaving half the calendar empty.

## Peoplestown: South Atlanta's Long Game

Peoplestown has a history that most Atlanta neighborhoods can't claim. It's one of Atlanta's oldest African American communities, with roots stretching back to the post-Civil War era, and it has survived the urban renewal pressures, highway construction, and disinvestment cycles that erased other south Atlanta neighborhoods. That resilience gives Peoplestown a genuine character — a sense of place that arrived before the investment did, and will outlast the current hype.

For STR owners, the case for Peoplestown is structural. Georgia State Stadium is the immediate demand driver — the former Turner Field has been transformed into a multi-use events complex that draws visitors throughout the year, with football season providing a predictable series of high-demand weekends when properties closer to Midtown or Buckhead are priced out of reach for many guests. Overflow demand from stadium events fills south Atlanta properties consistently, and Peoplestown's proximity to the stadium is a genuine differentiator.

The Georgia Avenue dining corridor in adjacent Summerhill extends the appeal. Halfway Crooks Beer has become one of Atlanta's most recommended craft beer destinations; Little Bear has earned national food press for its Lowcountry menu. Guests who want to walk to dinner after a game or event have options that make Peoplestown a complete stay rather than just a place to sleep.

The longer-term opportunity is the BeltLine Southside Trail. The planned trail will run along Peoplestown's perimeter, connecting the neighborhood to Grant Park, Summerhill, and eventually the full BeltLine loop. When that connection is complete, Peoplestown will have the same trail-access proposition that has driven sustained demand in every neighborhood the BeltLine has touched. Owners who establish now will have the review base and market positioning to capture that demand from day one.

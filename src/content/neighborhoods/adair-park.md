## Adair Park: Where Victorian Atlanta Meets the BeltLine

Adair Park occupies a genuinely special position on Atlanta's Westside. It's old enough to have the architectural character that guests pay a premium for, close enough to the BeltLine to have trail access that drives leisure bookings, and adjacent enough to Lee + White that craft beer and dinner are a short walk away on any given night. Not many Atlanta neighborhoods can say all three.

The Victorian homes are the visual anchor. Adair Park was built out primarily in the 1890s through 1910s, and a remarkable number of the original homes have survived. Queen Anne cottages, Folk Victorian bungalows, and Craftsman-era houses line the neighborhood's streets in a way that feels like Atlanta before the freeway era. For guests who come to Atlanta from cities without this kind of housing stock, staying in a genuine 1890s home is part of the experience — something worth booking specifically, not just where you sleep between attractions.

Lee + White and Monday Night Brewing define the walkable evening. Monday Night Brewing is one of Atlanta's most beloved craft breweries, and the Lee + White complex on Murphy Avenue SE has become a legitimate Westside destination for food, drink, and events. From an Adair Park property, that corridor is a pleasant walk along streets that give you the neighborhood rather than a parking garage. Guests who discover this don't just visit Lee + White — they often post about it, which means organic discovery for future bookings.

The BeltLine Westside Trail ties it together. Trail access connects Adair Park guests to a broader network of destinations and creates the kind of walkable-city experience that short-term rental guests increasingly seek.

## Perimeter: A Corporate-and-Medical Demand Engine in Sandy Springs

The Perimeter district is built for business. Centered on the I-285 and GA-400 interchange, it packs together one of metro Atlanta's densest clusters of corporate headquarters, the Pill Hill complex of major hospitals and medical centers, and Perimeter Mall as the retail anchor. MARTA rail and two major highways make it exceptionally easy to reach. For short-term rental owners, this is not a leisure or nightlife market — it's a year-round corporate-and-medical demand engine, and that distinction shapes everything about how a property should be positioned and run.

### Why Guests Come

Two forces dominate. The first is corporate: the district's headquarters and offices generate a constant stream of business travelers — employees, clients, visiting executives, trainees, and relocating professionals — many of whom prefer a furnished home with space and a kitchen over a hotel room, especially for multi-night and multi-week stays. The second is medical: the Pill Hill hospitals draw patients, the families accompanying them, and traveling medical professionals on assignment, all of whom need comfortable, often extended accommodations near the centers. Perimeter Mall adds a leisure-and-retail layer that supports longer stays, and the area's transit and highway access make it a convenient base for guests with business anywhere in the metro.

### Who Books Here

The guest mix is weighted toward longer, practical stays. Corporate travelers and relocation transferees book midweek and frequently for extended periods. Medical guests — patients, caregivers, and traveling staff — often need housing for weeks at a time and prioritize comfort, quiet, and proximity to the hospitals. This is a market where extended-stay and reliability matter far more than nightlife adjacency. Compact units can work for solo business travelers, but well-equipped homes that function for real, longer stays capture the most valuable bookings.

### What Makes It Work, and the Right Setup

Perimeter's defining advantage is the steadiness of corporate and medical demand, which produces weekday and year-round occupancy that purely seasonal markets can't match, plus longer reservations that cut turnover costs. To capture it, a property has to be set up for genuine stays: a dedicated workspace and fast, reliable internet for the corporate guest; quality bedding, blackout-capable bedrooms, and a full kitchen for medical and extended-stay guests; easy parking and access; and comfortable, quiet common areas.

## Downtown Buford: Historic Main Street, the Mall of Georgia, and Lake Lanier

Downtown Buford has more character than its location near a major mall might suggest. The historic main street, anchored by the Tannery Row Artist Colony and a growing dining scene, gives the area a genuine walkable core. But Buford's biggest short-term rental advantages sit just beyond downtown: the Mall of Georgia, one of the state's largest shopping and entertainment destinations, and Lake Lanier, with its boating, beaches, and lakeside recreation. For owners, that pairing of a characterful downtown with two heavyweight regional draws produces a steady and varied stream of guests.

### Why Guests Come

Lake Lanier is the seasonal engine. From spring through fall, the lake pulls families and groups for boating, beach days, and weekends on the water, and Buford's position at its doorstep makes it a natural base. The Mall of Georgia is a year-round draw, bringing shoppers — many from outside the immediate area — who turn a shopping trip into an overnight or weekend stay. Downtown itself adds a walkable layer of dining and the Tannery Row arts scene for guests who want character and a place to stroll. And the northeast corridor contributes event and corporate demand, with venues like Coolray Field and the I-85/I-985 business corridor nearby. The result is a market with strong seasonal peaks and a dependable year-round floor.

### Who Books Here

The guest mix leans toward families and groups. Lake-season visitors come for the water and want space — multiple bedrooms, room to gather, and ideally outdoor area. Shoppers book around Mall of Georgia trips. Families come for reunions, holidays, and special occasions, drawn by the area's spacious homes and lake access. Leisure guests use the historic downtown as a walkable base, and the corridor adds some weekday corporate and event demand. This is largely a market for comfortable, well-equipped homes that suit groups and longer family stays rather than compact units.

### What Makes It Work, and the Right Setup

Buford's strength is the blend of strong seasonal lake demand with year-round shopping and family-gathering bookings, which together keep the calendar from going quiet. The challenge is the seasonality: lake demand spikes in summer and softens in winter, so pricing has to be active rather than flat to capture peak weekends and fill the off-season. The properties that perform best position around their strongest draw — lake access, proximity to the Mall of Georgia, or walkability to downtown — and are set up for real family and group stays: comfortable common areas, quality bedding, a full kitchen, and outdoor space where possible, plus fast internet for any corporate guests.

The way people travel for work has changed. Remote work hasn't gone away — for a large and growing segment of the workforce, it's permanent. And the workers who can work from anywhere have discovered something obvious: "anywhere" can be a well-appointed Airbnb in Atlanta for a week, or a month, instead of a hotel or a home office.

For short-term rental owners, this shift represents a real opportunity — but only if the property is actually set up for it. A great apartment with slow wifi and a kitchen barstool as the "workspace" will earn a one-star mention in the review that's hard to undo.

## The Remote-Work Guest Segment Is Worth Understanding

Before investing in a setup, it helps to understand who you're attracting. Remote-work guests and workation travelers share a few defining traits:

They have functional requirements that traditional leisure guests don't prioritize — specifically wifi quality and workspace usability. They'll spend part of nearly every day working, which means they'll experience your workspace setup in a way that a weekend leisure guest simply won't.

They tend to book longer stays. A workation is typically a week or longer, often a month. Longer stays reduce turnover frequency, spread cleaning costs, and — managed well — produce steady revenue with less operational churn. For Atlanta properties near business corridors or desirable neighborhoods, this guest type can be a meaningful revenue stabilizer.

They're typically self-sufficient, quiet, and lower-drama compared to short-stay leisure groups. The same guest who needs a reliable workspace tends not to be throwing parties.

## Wifi: The Non-Negotiable Foundation

Everything else in a remote-work setup is secondary to a reliable internet connection. "Reliable" means two things: consistently fast, and consistently on.

Speed requirements for working guests:

| Use case | Minimum recommended down/up |
|---|---|
| Single guest, video calls + general work | 100 Mbps / 20 Mbps |
| Two guests working simultaneously | 200 Mbps / 50 Mbps |
| Multiple guests or heavy file transfer | 300+ Mbps / 100+ Mbps |

Speed alone isn't the full picture. Dead zones in the property, router hardware that hasn't been updated in years, and shared building internet with unpredictable congestion can all produce a guest experience that doesn't match the headline speed. A mesh wifi system (Eero, Google Nest, or similar) costs relatively little and eliminates the dead-zone problem in most residential properties.

Disclose your actual, tested speed in your listing. Guests who filter for remote-work properties will often ask — telling them upfront builds trust and reduces the risk of a review mention about connectivity.

## Building a Real Workspace

The difference between a listing that gets filtered out by remote-work guests and one that earns their loyalty often comes down to whether the workspace is genuinely usable for a full workday.

A proper workspace includes: a dedicated desk or table that isn't shared with dining, a chair with back support that a person can sit in for several hours, good task lighting that doesn't create screen glare, and accessible power. These are baseline requirements — meeting them puts you in the running.

What elevates a workspace:

- An external monitor (or a monitor stand that raises a laptop to eye level)
- A USB-C hub or multi-port charger for guests with multiple devices
- Blackout capability in the workspace area for daytime video calls
- Physical separation from sleeping areas — a workspace in a living room or dedicated office corner is meaningfully better than a desk at the foot of the bed

Placement matters. A desk positioned facing a window with natural light, away from the bedroom, signals that you thought about how someone would actually use the space — and that signal comes through in your listing photos.

## Amenity Checklist for Remote-Work Guests

Beyond wifi and the desk itself, remote workers notice a set of smaller details:

- **Coffee setup**: A good automatic drip or a Nespresso machine is standard; a pour-over kit or French press with quality beans is appreciated. People working from home start their mornings differently than vacation guests.
- **Comfortable seating for calls**: Not just a desk chair — somewhere to take a voice call informally, a couch or armchair positioned away from background noise.
- **Printer access or easy workaround**: Less commonly needed, but worth noting if you have one. Many remote workers have fully digital workflows, but a few will ask.
- **Quiet hours and environment**: Properties in residential neighborhoods with relatively low ambient noise have a genuine advantage here. Mention it in your listing.
- **Blackout curtains**: Critical for guests on calls with people in different time zones — early morning or late evening calls require light control.

Checkout cleaning expectations and mid-stay communication are also relevant to this guest type — remote workers staying for two or more weeks expect a mid-stay clean and clear communication about when to expect it. Addressing this proactively in your house rules prevents friction. ## Listing Your Property for Remote-Work Discovery

Airbnb's dedicated workspace filter is one of the few amenity filters that directly shapes search results for a specific guest type. Meeting the standard — a true dedicated workspace, not a kitchen table — and checking the dedicated workspace amenity in your listing unlocks this search traffic.

Beyond the filter, listing copy matters. Mentioning "reliable gigabit wifi," "a proper desk setup for full-day work," or "ideal for workations and extended stays" in your description reaches guests who are scanning for these signals before they even look at the photos.

## What This Looks Like in Practice

The properties that perform best with remote-work guests in Atlanta aren't the ones with the flashiest design — they're the ones where the fundamentals are right: you get on a video call without the connection dropping, the chair doesn't make your back ache by noon, and the coffee situation is sorted. These are achievable standards at modest cost, and they produce a guest type that tends to be extremely good for your review history and revenue stability.

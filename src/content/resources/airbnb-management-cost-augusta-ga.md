Short-term rental management in Augusta typically ranges from roughly 15% to 40% of gross booking revenue, depending on how much a company actually handles. Augusta occupies a singular position in the Georgia market: no other city has a demand event comparable to the Masters Tournament, and managing that week correctly can define a property's year. This guide explains how pricing works, what should be included, and how to judge whether a rate is worth it.

## How Augusta Short-Term Rental Pricing Works

Most managers charge a percentage of **gross booking revenue** — everything a guest pays before platform fees. Some quote against **net revenue**, which makes the same economics look cheaper, so confirm the basis before comparing quotes.

The percentage reflects service depth, and Augusta concentrates that depth into a single week more than almost any market in the country. The Masters is the most concentrated short-term rental demand event in Georgia. Getting it right — the right daily rates, the right minimum-night requirements, the right listing positioning relative to Augusta National — is the central skill of managing an Augusta property, and exactly where a capable manager justifies the fee.

## What a Full-Service Fee Should Include

For an Augusta property, full-service should bundle all of this without add-on charges:

- **Listing and marketing** — professional photography, optimized copy across major platforms, and positioning that speaks to golf groups during the Masters and to medical and event travelers year-round
- **Masters Week pricing** — daily rate strategy, minimum-night requirements, and listing optimization for the tournament, as part of the standard rate
- **Year-round pricing** — dynamic rates for medical-district, military, and event demand the rest of the year
- **Guest management** — screening, 24/7 communication, keyless check-in, and review handling
- **Operations and reporting** — vetted cleaning teams, restocking, maintenance coordination, monthly statements, and tax documentation

A quote that charges a Masters-Week surcharge — or excludes Masters pricing strategy — isn't truly full-service in this market.

## Full-Service vs. Cheap Base Rates

A low headline rate is tempting until you realize the most important week of the year carries an extra fee.

| Service Level | Typical Range | What's Included |
|---|---|---|
| Full-service management | 18–35% | Listing, Masters and year-round pricing, guests, cleaning, maintenance |
| Partial / co-hosting | 10–18% | Listing and pricing; owner runs local operations |
| Listing-only | 5–10% | Setup only |

Under a partial model, you're still coordinating cleaners and handling guests — including during the highest-stakes week of the year — while the manager bills for the listing alone. For owners who want passive income from a property whose biggest revenue is compressed into a single week, full-service is the model that delivers it.

## How to Evaluate Whether a Manager Is Worth It

Compare honestly. Self-managed: occupancy times nightly rate times available nights, minus your time, cleaning, and platform costs. Managed: professional pricing and positioning typically lift both occupancy and average rate, and in Augusta the difference between a well-managed Masters Week and a mishandled one can be substantial. We'd also caution against evaluating an Augusta investment on Masters Week alone — a property that earns well only that week and sits idle otherwise has a weaker return than one capturing the year-round base too. A good manager builds for both.

Then value your time. If you don't want to manage the most consequential week of your rental year yourself, the fee is the cost of that confidence. Ask any prospective manager how they handle Masters Week pricing and minimum nights, how they fill the rest of the calendar, and what their all-in rate excludes.

## Augusta's Market: Why Active Management Pays Off

Every April, Augusta National Golf Club hosts the most prestigious golf tournament in the world, and the city's entire accommodation infrastructure runs at capacity. Demand extends well beyond Augusta proper — North Augusta, Evans, Martinez, and the broader CSRA all see elevated rates. For owners who manage it correctly, Masters Week can represent a defining portion of annual revenue. Proximity to Augusta National is the primary value driver that week, and larger homes that accommodate groups of golf fans traveling together perform best.

But Augusta has real year-round demand that distinguishes it from a pure event market. AU Health / Augusta University Medical Center is one of the largest medical complexes in the Southeast, generating steady demand from patients in treatment, accompanying families, and visiting professionals. Fort Eisenhower brings military-affiliated travel. The Augusta Riverwalk and Augusta Canal National Heritage Area draw leisure visitors, the James Brown Arena hosts concerts and events, and the Augusta Futurity cutting-horse competition is a known January draw. A manager who prices the Masters as the peak and the medical-and-military base as the floor — while handling Augusta-Richmond County compliance — captures far more than a single-week approach.

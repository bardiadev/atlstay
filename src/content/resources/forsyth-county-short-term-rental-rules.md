Forsyth County wraps Lake Lanier's western and southern shores and centers on Cumming. It's one of the highest-income counties in the country and a strong family-travel and lake market — but its short-term rental rules aren't written in a single place, and the lake itself adds a layer most metro counties don't have. A property in the City of Cumming, a property in unincorporated Forsyth, and a lake-community property behind an HOA gate can each face a different reality.

This is a general orientation, not legal advice. Always verify current requirements directly with the relevant authority before listing.

## Forsyth County, Cumming, and the Lake Layer

Forsyth County government governs **unincorporated** Forsyth — land outside any city. The **City of Cumming** is an incorporated city with its own government, ordinances, and zoning. A property inside Cumming follows the city's rules; a property in unincorporated Forsyth follows the county's zoning. These are separate systems, and approval in one does not transfer to the other.

On top of that government layer sits a private one that matters enormously here: **HOA covenants in Lake Lanier communities**. Many of the lake's most desirable subdivisions — particularly gated and planned developments — restrict or prohibit short-term rentals, entirely independent of whatever the county or city allows.

Don't infer your jurisdiction from a mailing address or ZIP code. Use Forsyth County's property/GIS records, check your tax bill's taxing authority, and call the City of Cumming if there's any doubt about whether a parcel is inside the city.

## How Forsyth Generally Approaches STR

As across Georgia, the jurisdictions here regulate short-term rentals through a combination of **zoning**, **registration or permitting**, and **occupancy / hotel-motel tax**, with private **HOA covenants** layered on top.

Forsyth County has historically treated short-term rentals primarily through its **zoning code** rather than a single countywide STR license, and the City of Cumming applies its own framework within city limits. Because these rules have been revisited as lake demand and population have grown, treat any specific rule you read — including this one — as a starting point rather than a settled answer.

## The Lake Lanier Question

For Forsyth owners, the lake deserves its own checklist:

- **HOA covenants first.** In many lake communities, the covenant question decides everything. A government permit cannot override an HOA prohibition, so confirm with your association before anything else.
- **Shoreline and dock rules.** Lake Lanier's shoreline is federally managed, and dock permits and shoreline-use rules are governed separately from your STR. Don't assume guest dock or watercraft use is unrestricted.
- **Eligible lakefront is valuable.** Where a lake property *is* permitted, it can earn meaningful summer premiums — which is exactly why confirming eligibility before you invest is worth the effort.

## What to Verify Before You List in Forsyth

A practical due-diligence sequence:

**Step 1 — Confirm your exact jurisdiction.** City of Cumming or unincorporated Forsyth. Use parcel records, not assumptions.

**Step 2 — Read your HOA or condo covenants — early.** In Forsyth, and especially around the lake, this is frequently the deciding factor. Do it before you spend on anything.

**Step 3 — Contact the right government authority.** Ask Forsyth County, or the City of Cumming, what registration, permit, or zoning approval is currently required.

**Step 4 — Check zoning.** Confirm short-term rental use is allowed for your parcel.

**Step 5 — Understand your tax obligations.** Identify the state and local lodging taxes that apply, who collects them, and what you file yourself. A CPA who handles Georgia short-term rentals is worth consulting.

**Step 6 — Verify anything legal with a professional.** For covenant interpretation, ordinance questions, or tax filing, rely on an attorney or CPA.

Damage happens in short-term rentals. Not always, not even often with well-screened guests, but often enough that every host needs a systematic approach before something occurs — not after. The difference between a damage incident that resolves cleanly and one that becomes a prolonged, expensive dispute usually comes down to documentation, timing, and preparation.

This guide walks through how to document your property, how Airbnb's AirCover for Hosts program works in practice, and what prevention measures reduce the incidents worth worrying about. Nothing here is legal advice — for significant losses or complex situations, consult an attorney or insurance professional familiar with short-term rental operations.

## Documentation: The Foundation of Every Claim

No claim process — whether through AirCover, your insurance carrier, or the guest directly — works without a clear baseline. The baseline is your pre-stay documentation: thorough, timestamped photos of every room, surface, appliance, and item in the property taken immediately before each guest checks in.

"Immediately before" is not an exaggeration. Documentation taken a week before check-in is less useful because it can't definitively rule out changes between that date and the guest's arrival. The standard workflow:

1. Cleaning crew completes turnover and departs
2. Photos taken of every room, including walls, floors, furniture, kitchen appliances, bathroom fixtures, and outdoor spaces
3. Photos stored with the date/time stamp intact (most smartphone photos preserve this automatically)
4. Post-stay photos taken at the same level of thoroughness immediately after checkout

This process sounds tedious until you need it. A claim without a clear "before" is a claim that's difficult to win, because there's no way to definitively establish the guest caused the damage rather than it preexisting. Professional turnover teams that include documentation as a standard step remove this burden from the host and create a consistent record across every stay.

## How AirCover for Hosts Works

AirCover for Hosts is Airbnb's host protection program included with hosting on the platform. It provides damage protection for eligible bookings up to a stated coverage limit (Airbnb publishes current limits in their Help Center — always verify the current terms, as they are subject to update).

The claims process runs through Airbnb's Resolution Center and generally follows this sequence:

| Step | What happens |
|---|---|
| Discover damage after checkout | Document thoroughly before cleaning or any changes |
| File in Resolution Center | Submit within Airbnb's current claim window (verify in Help Center) |
| Provide documentation | Photos, description of damage, repair quotes or receipts |
| Airbnb reviews | May contact the guest; guest can respond |
| Resolution | Airbnb determines payout based on documentation and policy terms |

The most important practical detail: the claim window is short. Currently Airbnb requires claims to be submitted within 14 days of checkout or before the next guest checks in, whichever is earlier — but verify current timelines in Airbnb's Help Center before relying on this, as Airbnb updates its policies. Missing the window typically means the claim is ineligible, regardless of how clear the damage is.

Cost documentation matters as much as damage documentation. Airbnb requires specific, itemized evidence of cost — a formal repair quote from a contractor, a receipt for a replaced item — not a general estimate. Hosts who submit claims with organized, itemized documentation typically see faster resolution than those who submit general descriptions without cost support.

## What AirCover Does and Doesn't Cover

AirCover is most effective for clear, documented, guest-caused physical damage to specific property items. It is not a general-purpose insurance policy, and several categories are typically excluded or difficult to recover:

- **Normal wear and tear**: Expected deterioration from regular use is excluded. This is why "damage" claims for minor scuffs or gradual fabric wear rarely succeed.
- **Pre-existing damage**: Any damage that existed before the stay, or that can't be definitively ruled out as pre-existing, is at risk of being excluded. This is the baseline documentation problem again.
- **Consumables**: Toiletries, cleaning supplies, and similar consumables used during the stay aren't recoverable.
- **Lost items without clear evidence**: If a guest takes something, proving it definitively without both before-and-after documentation and a statement of what's missing is difficult.
- **Situations where the guest disputes your account**: AirCover gives guests an opportunity to respond, and in disputed cases, the quality of your documentation — photos, communication records, timestamps — determines who the facts support.

For high-value properties or owners who want coverage beyond what AirCover provides, a standalone short-term rental insurance policy is worth evaluating separately from the Airbnb program. Several insurers offer policies specifically designed for STR operations.

## Prevention Reduces the Claims You Have to File

The most effective damage management is avoiding the damage in the first place. The prevention levers that matter most:

**Guest screening**: Verified guests with positive review histories cause significantly less damage than unscreened, anonymous bookings. See our [guest screening guide](/resources/airbnb-guest-screening/) for a practical screening approach. The correlation between screening rigor and property condition is consistent.

**Clear house rules**: Rules that explicitly prohibit smoking indoors, address pet policies, and set occupancy limits reduce the most common damage categories. Guests who agree to these rules at booking have reduced grounds for disputing damage claims if they violate them.

**Smart physical setup**: Materials and furnishings that are durable, easily cleaned, and appropriate for rental use reduce both the frequency and the cost of damage. Removing irreplaceable personal items from rental properties is basic risk management. Smart locks with unique access codes for each guest eliminate key loss as a recurring issue and provide access records if a dispute arises.

**Professional turnover**: A thorough cleaning and documentation process between every stay catches minor damage before it becomes major, creates the before-and-after baseline that makes claims viable, and keeps the property in the condition that prevents guest frustration (which often drives retaliatory reviews alongside legitimate damage claims).

## After a Damage Incident

Once you've documented damage and filed a claim, the process largely follows Airbnb's timeline. During that period, communicate professionally and factually — through the Airbnb platform, not external channels — and let your documentation speak. Escalation and frustration in messages rarely help and can sometimes complicate the process.

After the claim resolves, review what happened. Was there a screening signal you could have weighted differently? A house rule that needed to be clearer? A material choice in the property that didn't hold up? Most experienced hosts find that damage incidents, when they happen, reveal a specific gap they can close.

Short-term rental management in Marietta typically ranges from roughly 15% to 40% of gross booking revenue, depending on how much a company actually handles. Marietta isn't a bedroom community — it's a city with its own gravitational pull, and several independent demand drivers that reward deliberate pricing. This guide explains how management pricing works, what should be included, and how to judge whether a rate is worth it.

## How Marietta Airbnb Management Pricing Works

Most managers charge a percentage of **gross booking revenue** — everything a guest pays before platform fees. Some quote against **net revenue**, which makes the same deal look cheaper, so confirm the basis before comparing quotes.

The percentage reflects service depth, and Marietta's depth is largely about its calendar. A property here can be booked by a Square weekend visitor, a Braves fan avoiding a post-game drive, a Kennesaw Mountain hiker, or a Lockheed contractor — sometimes in the same month. Pricing each of those demand events correctly is the work that distinguishes an active manager from a passive one.

## What a Full-Service Fee Should Include

For a Marietta property, full-service should bundle all of this without add-on charges:

- **Listing and marketing** — professional photography, optimized copy across major platforms, and positioning that speaks to leisure, sports, business, and heritage guests
- **Pricing** — dynamic rates with proactive spikes for the Braves and Battery Atlanta event calendar, Marietta Square events, and seasonal demand
- **Guest management** — screening, 24/7 communication, keyless check-in, and review handling
- **Operations** — vetted cleaning teams, linen and supply restocking, and maintenance coordination
- **Reporting** — monthly statements and tax documentation

A quote that excludes event-based pricing for Truist Park and the Square isn't truly full-service in this market.

## Full-Service vs. Cheap Base Rates

A low headline rate is tempting until you see what's left out.

| Service Level | Typical Range | What's Included |
|---|---|---|
| Full-service management | 18–35% | Listing, event pricing, guests, cleaning, maintenance |
| Partial / co-hosting | 10–18% | Listing and pricing; owner runs local operations |
| Listing-only | 5–10% | Setup only |

Under a partial model, you're still coordinating cleaners and handling guests — including on a packed Braves homestand weekend — while the manager bills for the listing alone. For owners who want passive income from a property that books across multiple demand streams, full-service is the model that actually delivers it.

## How to Evaluate Whether a Manager Is Worth It

Compare honestly. Self-managed: occupancy times nightly rate times available nights, minus your time, cleaning, and platform costs. Managed: professional pricing and photography typically lift both occupancy and average rate. In Marietta, the Braves play 81 regular-season home games plus any postseason, and the Battery hosts concerts and events year-round — a dense, predictable demand pattern that owners on default pricing routinely underprice.

Then value your time. If you don't want to manage a calendar with this many moving events, the fee is the cost of that simplicity. Ask any prospective manager how they price Truist Park and Square events, how they handle turnovers during busy weekends, and what their all-in rate excludes.

## Marietta's Market: Why Active Management Pays Off

Marietta rewards owners who understand its distinct personality. The civic anchor is Marietta Square — one of the genuine public squares that survived Atlanta's suburban expansion intact — whose restaurants, boutiques, and recurring events (the Marietta Market, seasonal festivals, Art in the Park) generate steady weekend demand. Guests booking near the Square often stay two or three nights to enjoy the walkable dining scene.

Beyond the Square, the demand is genuinely multi-layered. Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park is one of Georgia's most visited National Park sites, drawing history enthusiasts and hikers. Dobbins Air Reserve Base and Lockheed Martin bring business and contractor travelers year-round. And Truist Park, home of the Atlanta Braves, is just minutes away — with 81 home games plus playoff potential and a calendar of concerts and events at the surrounding Battery Atlanta, it's one of the most predictable demand generators in Cobb County. A manager who prices all of that deliberately, and keeps you compliant under Marietta's STR ordinance and Cobb County rules, captures far more than a flat metro rate would.

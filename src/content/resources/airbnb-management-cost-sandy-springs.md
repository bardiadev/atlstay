Short-term rental management in Sandy Springs typically ranges from roughly 15% to 40% of gross booking revenue, depending on how much a company actually handles. Sandy Springs is a business-first market, and business travel is the most predictable, high-frequency demand driver in the short-term rental world — which makes consistent, professional occupancy management especially valuable. This guide explains how pricing works, what should be included, and how to judge whether a rate is worth it.

## How Sandy Springs Airbnb Management Pricing Works

Most managers charge a percentage of **gross booking revenue** — everything a guest pays before platform fees. Some quote against **net revenue**, which makes the same economics look cheaper, so confirm the basis before comparing quotes.

The percentage reflects service depth. In a corporate market, depth means keeping the calendar full on weekdays — not just chasing weekend leisure peaks. A manager who configures a listing for extended-stay professionals and actively fills Tuesday-and-Wednesday gaps is doing the work that produces steady income here, which a passive, weekend-focused operator leaves on the table.

## What a Full-Service Fee Should Include

For a Sandy Springs property, full-service should bundle all of this without add-on charges:

- **Listing and marketing** — professional photography, optimized copy across major platforms, and configurations that appeal to corporate and leisure guests alike
- **Pricing** — dynamic rates calibrated to the corporate-travel calendar, with extended-stay rate logic and weekend leisure adjustments
- **Guest management** — screening, 24/7 communication, keyless check-in, and review handling
- **Operations** — vetted cleaning teams, linen and supply restocking, and maintenance coordination
- **Reporting** — monthly statements and tax documentation

A quote that treats the property as a pure weekend-leisure listing isn't truly full-service in a market this business-driven.

## Full-Service vs. Cheap Base Rates

A low headline rate is tempting until you see the exclusions.

| Service Level | Typical Range | What's Included |
|---|---|---|
| Full-service management | 18–35% | Listing, corporate and leisure pricing, guests, cleaning, maintenance |
| Partial / co-hosting | 10–18% | Listing and pricing; owner runs local operations |
| Listing-only | 5–10% | Setup only |

Under a partial model, you're still coordinating cleaners and handling guest issues — including for back-to-back corporate stays — while the manager bills for the listing alone. For owners who want passive income from a property that should be booking midweek as well as weekends, full-service is the model that delivers it.

## How to Evaluate Whether a Manager Is Worth It

Compare honestly. Self-managed: occupancy times nightly rate times available nights, minus your time, cleaning, and platform costs. Managed: professional pricing and photography typically lift both occupancy and average rate, and in Sandy Springs a manager who captures consistent midweek corporate demand can raise occupancy in a way that more than offsets the fee.

Then value your time. If you don't want to manage turnovers and guest communication for a property that books frequently throughout the week, the fee is the cost of that freedom. Ask any prospective manager how they fill midweek demand, how they configure listings for corporate guests, and what their all-in rate excludes.

## Sandy Springs' Market: Why Active Management Pays Off

Sandy Springs doesn't grab headlines like Midtown or Buckhead, but for short-term rental owners that relative quiet is an advantage. The market is anchored by Perimeter Center — one of the largest suburban office concentrations in the Southeast — where major companies across technology, finance, healthcare, and professional services maintain significant operations. That generates a steady stream of business travelers who prefer the comfort and value of a private rental to a generic hotel room, and crucially, that demand doesn't disappear during slow tourism seasons. It shows up on weeknights and during extended projects that run weeks rather than days.

A second stream rounds out the calendar: weekend leisure drawn to the Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area, Morgan Falls Overlook, and the dining and nightlife of nearby Buckhead. Larger single-family homes near Perimeter Center or the river serve both extended-stay corporate travelers and leisure groups, while well-furnished condos along Hammond Drive and Roswell Road suit solo business travelers. A manager who prices for this dual demand — and keeps you compliant under the city's own STR licensing framework, separate from Atlanta's — captures far more than a weekend-only approach.

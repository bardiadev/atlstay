Gwinnett County is metro Atlanta's second-most-populous county, and its short-term rental rules are not set in one place. A property in Duluth, a property in Lawrenceville, a property in Suwanee, a property in Buford, and a property in unincorporated Gwinnett can each fall under a different framework. For an owner, identifying the right one is the first and most important step.

This is a general orientation, not legal advice. Always verify current requirements directly with the relevant government authority before listing.

## One County, Many Sets of Rules

Gwinnett County government governs **unincorporated** Gwinnett — areas outside any city. But Gwinnett also contains numerous incorporated cities with their own governments and ordinances, including:

- The City of Duluth
- The City of Lawrenceville (the county seat)
- The City of Suwanee
- The City of Buford
- and others such as Norcross, Peachtree Corners, Snellville, and Sugar Hill

A property inside one of these cities follows that city's rules. A property in unincorporated Gwinnett follows the county's zoning. These are separate systems, and clearing one does not clear another.

Don't infer your jurisdiction from a mailing address or ZIP code — they cross city lines. Use Gwinnett County's property/GIS records, check your tax bill's taxing authority, and call the city directly if there's any doubt.

## How Gwinnett Generally Approaches STR

Like most of Georgia, Gwinnett's jurisdictions regulate short-term rentals through a mix of **zoning**, **registration or permitting**, and **occupancy / hotel-motel tax**, sitting alongside private **HOA covenants**.

A notable feature of Gwinnett is that several cities have been **actively moving on short-term rental ordinances** — Lawrenceville, Suwanee, and Duluth among those that have adopted or considered local rules — while **unincorporated Gwinnett** has leaned on its **zoning code** rather than a single countywide STR license. That means the regulatory picture varies meaningfully across the county and has been changing. A rule you confirm for one city may not match the city next door, and a rule that was current last year may have been updated since.

## What to Verify Before You List in Gwinnett

A practical due-diligence sequence:

**Step 1 — Confirm your exact jurisdiction.** Duluth, Lawrenceville, Suwanee, Buford, another Gwinnett city, or unincorporated county. Use parcel records, not assumptions.

**Step 2 — Contact the right authority.** Ask that specific government — the city's planning / community development office, or Gwinnett County — what registration, permit, or zoning approval is currently required.

**Step 3 — Check zoning.** Confirm short-term rental use is allowed for your parcel before spending on setup.

**Step 4 — Read your HOA or condo covenants.** Gwinnett associations frequently restrict short-term rentals. This is independent of any government rule, and it stops many otherwise-eligible properties.

**Step 5 — Understand your tax obligations.** Identify the state and local lodging taxes that apply, who collects them, and what you file yourself. Consider a CPA who handles Georgia short-term rentals.

**Step 6 — Verify anything legal with a professional.** For ordinance interpretation or tax questions, rely on an attorney or CPA, not a blog post.

Short-term rental regulation across Gwinnett is genuinely in motion, with cities adopting and refining ordinances. **Rules change frequently — confirm current requirements with the City of Duluth, Lawrenceville, Suwanee, or Buford as applicable, or with Gwinnett County, before listing your property or renewing any registration.** Don't rely on a figure or rule from a blog post, including this one, as your final word.

The pet-friendly question comes up early for most Atlanta Airbnb hosts, and the answer is rarely obvious. There's genuine demand on one side and genuine wear on the other — and the right decision depends on your property type, your flooring, your cleaning operation, and your tolerance for variable turnover costs.

This guide lays out the real tradeoffs so you can make the decision with clear eyes rather than optimism or anxiety.

## The Demand Case for Going Pet-Friendly

A meaningful share of the traveling population has pets, and a subset of those travelers filters specifically for pet-friendly listings before they look at anything else. For Atlanta specifically — a market that draws a high proportion of domestic travelers arriving by car — the pet-friendly filter carries more weight than in markets dominated by air travel.

The practical implication: a pet-friendly listing shows up in searches that a pet-free listing doesn't. In a crowded market, visibility matters. Hosts who allow pets access a guest segment that has fewer options and is often willing to pay a premium for the convenience. This shows up in both occupancy and average daily rate for properties that handle the logistics correctly.

## The Real Costs: Wear, Cleaning, and Incidents

The demand case is real. So is the wear case.

Pets — dogs especially — generate incremental cleaning and maintenance costs that a flat pet fee may or may not cover depending on the stay. Scratched hardwood, stained carpet, odor embedded in upholstery, fur in hard-to-clean crevices — these are the routine costs. On a bad stay, there's property damage that exceeds a deposit.

The honesty check: if your property has original hardwood floors you'd be devastated to see scratched, or light-colored carpet throughout, or soft furnishings that would be difficult to replace, the wear calculation looks different than it does for a property with durable LVP, tile, and minimal soft surfaces.

## Setting Up a Pet-Friendly Property Right

If you go pet-friendly, the setup matters. Hosts who do this well treat pet-friendly as a deliberate operational choice, not an afterthought.

**Flooring first.** Hard flooring is close to a prerequisite for a pet-friendly property that performs well over time. Luxury vinyl plank is the most practical choice — durable, water-resistant, scratch-resistant, and replaceable in sections. If your current flooring is carpet throughout, a flooring upgrade before going pet-friendly often pays for itself through reduced replacement costs and better reviews.

**Furniture selection.** Low-profile, easy-to-clean furniture in darker or mid-tone upholstery performs better than light-colored or delicate pieces. Leather and performance fabric hold up better than linen or open-weave textiles. Some hosts designate specific furniture as "pet-approved" and keep other pieces in the property for non-pet stays — this gets complicated with turnover but works for some property types.

**Cleaning protocol.** Your cleaning team needs a pet-specific protocol, not just a general clean. Enzyme-based cleaners for odor on hard surfaces, inspection of soft goods after every pet stay, and airing out the unit are all standard steps. Our [Atlanta turnover cleaning standards](/resources/airbnb-turnover-cleaning-standards-atlanta/) covers what professional-grade turnover looks like in practice.

**Outdoor setup.** If your property has outdoor space, a defined area for pets — and a note in your listing that guests are responsible for cleanup — keeps the yard functional for all future guests.

## Fees, Deposits, and Rules: Getting the Structure Right

A pet-friendly listing without a clear structure is a pet-friendly listing that loses money. The fee and deposit setup should:

- Cover the realistic incremental cleaning cost on a typical pet stay
- Provide a mechanism for addressing damage that exceeds normal wear
- Be clearly stated in the listing so guests know what to expect

| Structure element | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Flat pet fee (per stay) | Covers incremental cleaning on typical stays |
| Nightly pet surcharge | Scales cost with longer stays |
| Refundable damage deposit | Provides recourse for damage beyond normal wear |
| Weight / species limits | Controls exposure on atypical pets |
| Pet count limit | Reduces compound wear from multiple animals |
| Explicit outdoor rules | Manages yard condition for future guests |

Most hosts who allow pets set some combination of weight limits, species limits (dogs and cats only is common), and a cap on the number of animals. This keeps the liability manageable while still capturing the demand you're after.

## Pet-Friendly and Dynamic Pricing

One underappreciated aspect of going pet-friendly: it can smooth gaps in occupancy that non-pet properties leave open, particularly during shoulder seasons when leisure demand softens. A [dynamic pricing](/dynamic-pricing/) strategy that pairs well with a pet-friendly policy captures demand when it exists rather than leaving the calendar dark.

The combination — right audience, right price, right nights — is where the revenue upside of pet-friendly becomes clearest. It's not just about filling nights at any rate; it's about the right guests at the right price point.

## Is Pet-Friendly Right for Your Property?

The honest answer is that it depends on your property's durability, your location, and your operational setup. Properties with hard flooring, performance fabrics, and a professional cleaning team that handles pet-specific turnover are natural fits. Properties with irreplaceable original hardwood, white linen, and minimal cleaning support are harder cases.

The demand is there. The question is whether your specific property can handle it in a way that keeps your reviews strong and your costs predictable.

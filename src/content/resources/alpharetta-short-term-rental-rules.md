Alpharetta is one of the strongest short-term rental markets in north metro Atlanta — a concentration of major tech and finance employers, Avalon's year-round energy, and a packed Ameris Bank Amphitheatre concert calendar. But before any of that demand matters, an owner has to get the regulatory basics right. Alpharetta is its own city with its own rules, and they are not the same as the City of Atlanta's.

This is a general orientation, not legal advice. Always verify current requirements directly with the City of Alpharetta before listing.

## Alpharetta Sets Its Own Rules

The City of Alpharetta is an incorporated city within Fulton County, with its own government, its own ordinances, and its own zoning and code enforcement. It has adopted its own short-term rental framework — distinct from Atlanta's, distinct from unincorporated Fulton's, and distinct from neighboring cities like Roswell and Milton.

That means two things for an owner:

1. **Don't import Atlanta's rules.** Atlanta's licensing program, its caps, and its fees are Atlanta's. They tell you nothing reliable about Alpharetta.
2. **Confirm your jurisdiction.** Most "Alpharetta" addresses sit inside the city, but mailing addresses can be misleading near the edges. If there's any doubt, confirm with the city whether your parcel is inside Alpharetta's limits or in unincorporated Fulton.

## How Alpharetta Generally Approaches STR

Like most Georgia jurisdictions, Alpharetta regulates short-term rentals through a combination of **registration or permitting**, **zoning**, **safety standards**, and **occupancy / hotel-motel tax**, with private **HOA covenants** sitting alongside all of it.

In general terms, owners are expected to **register with the city**, ensure the property meets applicable **safety requirements**, and operate consistently with **zoning**. The specific mechanics — what documentation is required, what it costs, how renewal works, and what operating standards apply — have evolved as Alpharetta's market has grown. Treat any specific figure or rule you read, including this one, as a starting point to confirm rather than a settled answer.

## What to Verify Before You List in Alpharetta

A practical due-diligence sequence:

**Step 1 — Confirm you're in the City of Alpharetta.** Not unincorporated Fulton or a neighboring city. Use parcel records if there's any doubt.

**Step 2 — Contact the City of Alpharetta Community Development department.** Ask what registration, permit, zoning approval, and safety requirements currently apply to a short-term rental at your address.

**Step 3 — Check zoning.** Confirm short-term rental use is allowed for your parcel before you spend on furnishing or photography.

**Step 4 — Read your HOA or condo covenants.** Many Alpharetta communities restrict short-term rentals. This is independent of any city registration and frequently decides whether a property can operate at all.

**Step 5 — Understand your tax obligations.** Identify the state and local lodging taxes that apply, who collects them, and what you file yourself. A CPA who handles Georgia short-term rentals is worth consulting.

**Step 6 — Verify anything legal with a professional.** For ordinance interpretation or tax questions, rely on an attorney or CPA.

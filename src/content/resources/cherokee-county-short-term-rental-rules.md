Cherokee County is one of the fastest-growing counties in Georgia, and its short-term rental rules are not written in a single place. A property in Canton, a property in Woodstock, and a property in unincorporated Cherokee can each fall under a different framework. For an owner, the first job is simply figuring out which one applies.

This is a general orientation, not legal advice. Always verify current requirements directly with the relevant authority before listing.

## Cherokee County Is Not One Jurisdiction

Cherokee County government governs **unincorporated** Cherokee — land outside any city. But the county also contains incorporated cities with their own governments and ordinances, including:

- The City of Canton (the county seat)
- The City of Woodstock
- and smaller municipalities such as Holly Springs, Ball Ground, and Waleska

A property inside one of these cities follows that city's rules; a property in unincorporated Cherokee follows the county's zoning. These are separate systems, and approval in one does not transfer to another.

Don't assume your jurisdiction from a mailing address — addresses and ZIP codes cross city lines. Use Cherokee County's property/GIS records, check your tax bill's taxing authority, and call the city directly if there's any doubt.

## How Cherokee Generally Approaches STR

As across Georgia, the jurisdictions here regulate short-term rentals through a combination of **zoning**, **registration or permitting**, and **occupancy / hotel-motel tax**, layered on top of private **HOA covenants**.

Cherokee County has historically treated short-term rentals primarily through its **zoning code** rather than a single countywide STR license. The cities — Canton and Woodstock in particular — apply their own ordinances and zoning, which may include local registration and rules around guest counts, parking, and quiet hours. Because Cherokee is growing quickly and its jurisdictions have revisited these rules as demand has risen, treat any specific rule you read — including this one — as a starting point rather than a settled answer.

## What to Verify Before You List in Cherokee

A practical due-diligence sequence:

**Step 1 — Confirm your exact jurisdiction.** Canton, Woodstock, another Cherokee city, or unincorporated county. Use parcel records, not assumptions.

**Step 2 — Contact the right authority.** Ask that specific government — the city's planning / community development office, or Cherokee County — what registration, permit, or zoning approval is currently required.

**Step 3 — Check zoning.** Confirm short-term rental use is allowed for your parcel before spending on furnishing or photography.

**Step 4 — Read your HOA or condo covenants.** Many Cherokee communities, especially newer subdivisions, restrict short-term rentals. This is independent of any government rule.

**Step 5 — Understand your tax obligations.** Identify the state and local lodging taxes that apply, who collects them, and what you file yourself. A CPA who handles Georgia short-term rentals is worth consulting.

**Step 6 — Verify anything legal with a professional.** For ordinance interpretation or tax questions, an attorney or CPA is the right source.

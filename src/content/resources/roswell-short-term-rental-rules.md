Roswell is one of North Fulton's anchor cities — a historic downtown, an established affluent residential base, and steady suburban and corporate travel demand. For a short-term rental owner, the opportunity is real, but it starts with getting the regulatory basics right. Roswell is its own city with its own rules, and they are not the same as the City of Atlanta's, even though both sit inside Fulton County.

This is a general orientation, not legal advice. Always verify current requirements directly with the City of Roswell before listing.

## Roswell Sets Its Own Rules

The City of Roswell is an incorporated city within Fulton County, with its own government, ordinances, zoning, and code enforcement. It applies its own short-term rental framework — distinct from Atlanta's, distinct from unincorporated Fulton's, and distinct from neighboring cities like Sandy Springs, Alpharetta, and Milton.

For an owner, that means two things:

1. **Don't import Atlanta's rules.** Atlanta's licensing program, caps, and fees are Atlanta's. They tell you nothing reliable about Roswell.
2. **Confirm your jurisdiction.** Most "Roswell" addresses sit inside the city, but mailing addresses can mislead near the edges. If there's any doubt, confirm with the city whether your parcel is inside Roswell or in unincorporated Fulton.

## How Roswell Generally Approaches STR

Like most Georgia jurisdictions, Roswell regulates short-term rentals through a combination of **registration or permitting**, **zoning**, **safety standards**, and **occupancy / hotel-motel tax**, with private **HOA covenants** sitting alongside all of it. Roswell also has a **historic district and preservation overlays** that can add another layer for affected properties.

The specific mechanics — what documentation is required, what registration costs, how renewal works, what operating standards apply, and how historic overlays affect use — can change. Treat any specific figure or rule you read, including this one, as a starting point to confirm rather than a settled answer.

## The Historic District Layer

Roswell's historic core is one of its defining features, and it comes with its own considerations:

- **Design review and use restrictions** can apply in historic or overlay districts, on top of the city's general short-term rental rules.
- **Modifications** to a historic-district property — even ones aimed at improving an STR — may require additional review.
- **Confirm your overlay status** with the City of Roswell. Don't assume a property is or isn't in the historic district based on how "old" the neighborhood looks.

## What to Verify Before You List in Roswell

A practical due-diligence sequence:

**Step 1 — Confirm you're in the City of Roswell.** Not unincorporated Fulton or a neighboring city. Use parcel records if there's any doubt.

**Step 2 — Contact the City of Roswell Community Development department.** Ask what registration, permit, zoning approval, and safety requirements currently apply — and whether your address sits in a historic or overlay district.

**Step 3 — Check zoning.** Confirm short-term rental use is allowed for your parcel before spending on setup.

**Step 4 — Read your HOA or condo covenants.** Many Roswell communities restrict short-term rentals. This is independent of any city registration and often decides whether a property can operate.

**Step 5 — Understand your tax obligations.** Identify the state and local lodging taxes that apply, who collects them, and what you file yourself. A CPA who handles Georgia short-term rentals is worth consulting.

**Step 6 — Verify anything legal with a professional.** For ordinance interpretation, historic-overlay questions, or tax filing, rely on an attorney or CPA.

## Dalton: The Carpet Capital's Quiet Vacation Rental Opportunity

Dalton does not appear on most Georgia vacation rental target lists — and that is exactly why it represents a real opportunity for owners who understand it. The city of 35,000 in Whitfield County has an industrial identity so dominant it shapes the entire local economy: flooring. Not just Georgia flooring, not just Southern flooring — approximately 80% of the carpet produced in the United States comes from within a 65-mile radius of Dalton. Shaw Industries, the world's largest carpet manufacturer, is headquartered here. Mohawk Industries, the world's largest flooring manufacturer, is headquartered one county south.

### Structural Corporate Demand

The flooring industry's concentration in Dalton creates a type of STR demand that is almost entirely absent from Georgia's leisure-market cities: persistent, weather-independent, year-round corporate travel. Flooring buyers from major retailers, architects and interior designers who come to meet manufacturers, international trade delegations, sales teams visiting Shaw and Mohawk supplier facilities — this corporate traffic is real, recurring, and constitutes a demand base that does not disappear when it rains.

Corporate guests are among the best STR guests for several reasons beyond their consistency: they treat properties carefully, they book through platforms with professional travel policies, and they often extend stays when projects run long. A property positioned for this audience — extended-stay amenities, workspace, proximity to I-75 and the manufacturing corridors — can achieve occupancy rates that bear little resemblance to the leisure-only markets Dalton is usually lumped with.

### Civil War Heritage and Appalachian Access

Dalton's industrial identity coexists with a historical significance that draws a different audience entirely. In 1864, the city was the staging ground for the Atlanta Campaign — General Sherman's advance from Chattanooga southward began here with the Battle of Rocky Face Ridge and the flanking maneuver at Resaca that forced Confederate General Johnston to retreat from the "Gateway to the Deep South." The Dalton area's Civil War sites are part of a well-documented battlefield trail that connects to the Chickamauga park (25 miles north) and Kennesaw Mountain (50 miles south).

The Blue Ridge foothills east of Dalton provide the Conasauga River fishery, Cohutta Wilderness backpacking, and access to the Appalachian Trail — a different visitor profile who comes not for the factories but for some of the most biodiverse mountain streams in the eastern United States.

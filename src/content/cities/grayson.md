## Grayson's Short-Term Rental Market: Southeast Gwinnett's Growth Corridor

Grayson is a small city that most Atlanta visitors wouldn't recognize by name, which is precisely why it offers a certain STR opportunity that higher-profile Gwinnett markets don't: consistent, under-served family demand in a growth corridor that hasn't attracted heavy short-term rental competition.

### Southeast Gwinnett's Residential Growth Engine

Grayson sits at the heart of southeast Gwinnett's residential expansion zone. The area around Grayson, Dacula, and Auburn has absorbed significant household growth over the past decade, drawn by school district quality, lower housing costs relative to the county's north, and Hwy 316 access to the broader metro. That growth creates STR demand from people in the process of relocating — families who need weeks or months of temporary housing while their new construction is completed, corporate assignees scoping out the area before signing a lease, and visitors staying with family who have moved to the area.

### Relocation and Extended-Stay Demand

Extended-stay is an underappreciated STR segment in growth-corridor markets. When large volumes of families are relocating into a specific area, the demand for furnished temporary housing follows directly. A Grayson property configured for longer stays — full kitchen, washer and dryer, comfortable workspace, good parking — captures this demand in a way that nightly leisure listings don't.

### A Functional Market at an Accessible Price Point

Grayson doesn't offer the event-night spikes of Sugar Hill or the tech-company demand of Peachtree Corners, but it offers something equally valuable for certain owners: a realistic, manageable market with a consistent demand base and acquisition costs below the northern Gwinnett tier. For owners who already hold a Grayson property or who are evaluating southeast Gwinnett for STR investment, the fundamentals are solid.

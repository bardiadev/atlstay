## Why Sandy Springs Is One of Metro Atlanta's Most Reliable STR Markets

Sandy Springs doesn't make the headlines the way Midtown or Buckhead do, but for short-term rental owners who understand the market, that relative quiet is an advantage. This is a city built for business — and business travel is the most predictable, high-frequency demand driver in the STR world.

### The Corporate Demand Engine

Perimeter Center is one of the largest suburban office concentrations in the Southeast. Major companies across technology, finance, healthcare, and professional services maintain significant operations here, generating a steady stream of business travelers who prefer the comfort and value of a private rental to a generic hotel room. That demand doesn't disappear during slow tourism seasons. It shows up on Tuesday nights, Thursday mornings, and during extended projects that last weeks rather than days.

The result for well-managed Sandy Springs properties is a demand profile that evens out the peaks and valleys common in purely leisure-driven markets. Corporate guests also tend to be lower-maintenance, higher-rated, and more likely to book again.

### Leisure Demand: The Chattahoochee Factor

Sandy Springs borders the Chattahoochee River National Recreation Area, a string of parks and trails that draws kayakers, hikers, and families year-round. Morgan Falls Overlook Park and the Island Ford unit of the national recreation area bring visitors who want to stay close to the water rather than battle Buckhead traffic from a downtown hotel. Summer weekends especially see demand from groups making the Chattahoochee the centerpiece of a short Atlanta getaway.

### What Homes Perform Here

Sandy Springs has a residential character that sets it apart from denser intown neighborhoods. Homes with private outdoor space — a screened porch, a small yard, a pool — appeal strongly to the family and leisure segment. Properties near the Perimeter Center node with home-office amenities or fast Wi-Fi attract extended-stay professionals. The strongest performers tend to be properties that clearly serve one of these two profiles rather than trying to be everything.

Sandy Springs governs short-term rentals under its own city code.

## Senoia's Short-Term Rental Market: Film Tourism Capital of the South Metro

Senoia is a genuinely unusual market. Most small towns with historic downtowns have character but modest demand. Senoia has both — but the demand driver is not heritage tourism in the conventional sense. It's a globally recognized television series that turned this Coweta County community into an international destination.

### The Walking Dead and the Downtown Transformation

When The Walking Dead began filming in Senoia, the city's downtown had approximately eight businesses. Today it has more than fifty, with a waiting list for new commercial space. Nic & Norman's restaurant — opened by actor Norman Reedus and producer Greg Nicotero — operates in the heart of downtown. The Woodbury Shoppe, selling Walking Dead memorabilia, is owned by series creator Robert Kirkman and executive producers. The businesses are real, the ownership is verifiable, and the visitors are consistent.

The Georgia Tour Company has been running two-hour Walking Dead filming location tours through downtown Senoia since 2014 and estimates over 15,000 annual tour-takers. That number represents only the organized tour segment — independent visitors exploring the locations without a guide add substantially more to the total.

### Beyond the Show: A Self-Sustaining Destination

Senoia's smart historic preservation work — including design guidelines established in 2005 — ensured that the Walking Dead tourism boom built on top of an authentic foundation rather than replacing it. The downtown's antebellum and Victorian architecture is genuine; the sidewalk furniture, landscaping, and commercial mix give the district a livability that persists between tour groups.

Productions seeking well-preserved Southern small-town settings continue to use Senoia regularly, and the Georgia film ecosystem centered on nearby Trilith Studios ensures ongoing production activity in the region.

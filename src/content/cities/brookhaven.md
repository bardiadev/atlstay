## Brookhaven's Short-Term Rental Market: Medical Travel, Culinary Tourism, and Urban Convenience

Brookhaven was incorporated as a city in 2012 and quickly became one of the most sought-after addresses in DeKalb County. The combination of established residential neighborhoods, genuine urban walkability, and a location at the intersection of multiple demand generators makes it one of the more compelling STR markets in the entire metro.

### Children's Healthcare and the Medical-Family Guest

One of the most underappreciated demand drivers in the Brookhaven market is Children's Healthcare of Atlanta at Scottish Rite, which sits just south of the city limits in Sandy Springs and draws families from across Georgia and beyond. When a child is receiving care at one of the nation's leading pediatric hospitals, families need housing that isn't a hotel: they need a kitchen to prepare meals, a living room where the family can decompress together, and the kind of space that a 90-square-foot hotel room simply doesn't provide.

Short-term rental properties positioned for medical-family stays serve a guest segment that tends to be careful, appreciative, and often extending their stay longer than initially planned. This is demand that doesn't follow the typical leisure or corporate calendar — it's a consistent booking pool throughout the year.

### Buford Highway: Destination Dining Tourism

Buford Highway is internationally known among food travelers as one of the most diverse culinary corridors in North America. The stretch running through Brookhaven and Chamblee hosts Vietnamese, Korean, Chinese, Mexican, and dozens of other cuisines that draw serious food enthusiasts from Atlanta and around the region. Guests who center a weekend trip around Buford Highway dining specifically seek accommodation near the corridor — a positioning opportunity that Brookhaven properties can own if their listings communicate it clearly.

### The CDC and Academic Demand

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention campus and Emory University's Clairmont-area facilities generate professional and academic travel demand that fills weekday gaps. Researchers, visiting faculty, and contractors on extended projects are a reliable segment for properties within reasonable distance of these institutions.

### MARTA and the Car-Free Traveler

Brookhaven's MARTA access sets it apart from most metro-Atlanta suburbs. Guests arriving at Hartsfield-Jackson who want to avoid renting a car can reach Brookhaven directly on the Gold Line — a real selling point for international visitors and urban travelers. Properties near the Brookhaven/Oglethorpe or Chamblee stations can be marketed to this segment.

Local STR rules fall under Brookhaven's own city ordinance, separate from DeKalb County and Atlanta. For broader regulatory context, see our [Atlanta-area STR guide](/resources/atlanta-short-term-rental-regulations/).

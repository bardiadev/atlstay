## Lawrenceville's Short-Term Rental Market: Institutional Stability Meets a Reviving Downtown

Lawrenceville occupies a structural advantage that most suburban Atlanta markets lack: its demand is anchored by institutions, not just leisure. As the seat of Gwinnett County, the city hosts a constant flow of visitors whose reasons for staying have nothing to do with concerts or weekends away.

### The Medical Travel Engine

Northside Hospital Gwinnett on Medical Center Boulevard is one of the largest hospitals in the northeast Atlanta metro — a 696-bed Level II Trauma Center with specialized cardiovascular and women's health programs. Hospitals of this size generate continuous accommodation demand: traveling nurses on multi-week contracts, out-of-area patients receiving extended care, and family members who need a real home rather than a hotel room for days at a time.

For Keystone Stays clients in Lawrenceville, the medical-travel segment offers mid-week occupancy stability that purely leisure-driven markets don't have. A well-positioned property near the Medical Center Blvd corridor can serve a travel nurse on a 13-week contract just as readily as a leisure family arriving Friday afternoon.

### Gwinnett Justice Center and Legal-Travel Demand

The Gwinnett Justice & Administration Center at 75 Langley Drive is the operational hub of one of Georgia's largest county governments. Courts, hearings, and county agency business draw attorneys, witnesses, families, and county employees into Lawrenceville on a continuous basis — not just during high-profile trials but across the ordinary calendar of civil and criminal proceedings.

This legal-travel segment is small but highly consistent, and it overlaps naturally with other institutional demand generators. A property that works for a visiting attorney one week can host a medical-travel guest the next.

### The Historic Square and Downtown Revitalization

Lawrenceville's historic courthouse square — anchored by the 1885 Gwinnett Historic Courthouse, now a county landmark and event venue — is the center of a genuine downtown revitalization. Independent restaurants, boutique retail, and community events have made the area a weekend destination in its own right. Proximity to the square adds a leisure-demand dimension to what is already a strong institutional market.

Proximity to Gas South District in nearby Duluth extends Lawrenceville's event-night upside further.

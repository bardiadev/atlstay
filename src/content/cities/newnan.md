## Newnan's Short-Term Rental Market: Antebellum Heritage and I-85 Corridor Demand

Newnan offers a demand combination that most metro Atlanta suburbs don't achieve: a genuinely distinctive historic identity layered on top of a growing corporate and industrial employment base. Each segment benefits the other by filling different parts of the weekly and seasonal calendar.

### Six Historic Districts and a Preserved Downtown

Newnan's survival through the Civil War — combined with sustained investment in preservation over subsequent generations — produced an unusually intact built environment. Six separate National Register of Historic Places designations cover the range of the city's architectural heritage. Downtown Newnan's courthouse square, lined with locally owned restaurants and boutique retail in buildings that have stood for well over a century, is not a restoration project. It's the original fabric.

Heritage travelers who seek this character are a growing segment within the domestic travel market. They plan trips to specific places for specific reasons, book further in advance than leisure travelers, and care about authenticity in a way that rewards genuinely historic locations. A Newnan property near the historic districts is marketing something real.

### The I-85 Demand Layer

About 40 miles southwest of Atlanta on I-85, Newnan sits at the edge of the metro's expansion and the beginning of Coweta County's industrial and logistics development. Manufacturing operations, distribution facilities, and corporate campuses along this corridor generate consistent business travel that doesn't have a natural hotel base. Quality STR accommodations that serve the business traveler fill a gap that chain hotels leave open.

### A Market Ahead of Saturation

Newnan's STR market remains underserved relative to its demand drivers. That window of early-mover advantage won't be permanent as the southwest corridor continues to grow.

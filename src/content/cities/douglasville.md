## Douglasville's Short-Term Rental Market: State Park, Festival, and Film Demand

Douglasville has been doing something interesting over the past decade: quietly becoming a destination city while being positioned primarily as a commuter suburb. The STR market opportunity lies exactly at that intersection.

### Sweetwater Creek State Park: A Natural Anchor

Sweetwater Creek State Park is one of the most compelling natural destinations in metro Atlanta. The preserved ruins of the New Manchester Manufacturing Company — a Civil War-era textile mill burned by Sherman's troops — sit at the edge of the creek with a trail system that winds through old-growth forest and connects to multiple trailheads. The 2,549-acre park draws hikers, trail runners, nature photographers, and families who have driven past it on I-20 for years before finally visiting.

For STR owners, the park creates outdoor recreation demand that persists year-round. Unlike event-driven demand that concentrates in specific weeks, state park visitors come on weekends throughout every season — and they need accommodation that the park itself doesn't provide.

### Stranger Things and the Film Tourism Layer

Douglasville's downtown served as a filming location for Stranger Things, and the city hosts an annual festival that draws costumed fans from across the country to walk the same streets where the show filmed. This is film tourism in miniature: smaller than Senoia's Walking Dead economy, but genuine, growing, and nationally promoted.

### Lionsgate and the Production Economy

Lionsgate Studios' 2023 entry into Douglas County brought institutional film production to a market that previously had no studio infrastructure. As production activity accumulates, crew housing demand follows. Douglasville's proximity to Atlanta — about 25 miles — makes it accessible to the broader metro production ecosystem while offering more space and lower cost than properties closer to the urban core.

## Lake Hartwell: A 56,000-Acre Opportunity on the Georgia-South Carolina Border

Lake Hartwell is not well known outside the Southeast, but among anglers, boaters, and college football fans in Georgia and the Carolinas, it occupies a central place. The J. Strom Thurmond Dam created the reservoir in 1962 by flooding the Seneca River valley across the Georgia-South Carolina state line. The result is a lake nearly three times the size of Lake Lanier by surface area — 56,000 acres, approximately 962 miles of shoreline — with the college town of Clemson, South Carolina on its northeastern shore.

### The Clemson Effect

No single factor differentiates the Lake Hartwell STR market from other Georgia lake markets more sharply than Clemson University. Clemson's Tiger football program is one of the most followed college football brands in the South, and Death Valley stadium — less than 20 miles from the Georgia state line — hosts some of the loudest and most loyal fan bases in the SEC. On home game Saturdays, the entire region around Lake Hartwell elevates in demand.

Georgia-side lake houses position as Clemson-game bases — you get the full lake house experience, you're close enough to drive to the game, and you're in Georgia's less-congested lake corridor. For Clemson alumni families and extended fan groups, a private lake home beats a hotel block in every meaningful way. Managing that pricing spike correctly — capturing the premium without blocking last-minute regulars — is one of the most important calendar decisions of the Hartwell rental year.

### Summer on One of the Southeast's Largest Lakes

Hartwell's size is both its most compelling feature and its most undermarketed characteristic. The lake has enough room to absorb significant recreational traffic without the crowded-weekend feeling that plagues smaller Georgia lakes in July. Fishing, wakeboarding, pontoon boating, and shoreline exploration all have room to breathe. Anglers in particular find Hartwell productive — the lake has a strong largemouth bass fishery and hosts professional bass tournaments that draw their own travel demand.

Memorial Day through Labor Day is the lake's commercial peak. Fourth of July on Hartwell, with the lake's broad open water and multiple municipal fireworks visible from the water, is the annual high-demand ceiling. Well-positioned waterfront properties with dock access can achieve their strongest nightly rates of the year over this holiday weekend.

## Stockbridge's Short-Term Rental Market: Gateway, Nature, and Transit Convergence

Stockbridge is routinely described as Henry County's gateway — the point where I-75 and I-675 meet before separating southward into the county proper. That infrastructure reality shapes its STR market in concrete ways.

### A Dual-Interstate Advantage

Most suburban markets sit on one major highway artery. Stockbridge sits where two meet. That convergence gives any property here genuinely unusual geographic reach: I-75 corridor travelers coming from Atlanta to the south, and I-675 travelers approaching from Clayton County and the southeast all find Stockbridge a logical stopping point. Business visitors whose projects extend across both corridors — distribution, logistics, construction project management — find a Stockbridge base more efficient than choosing either corridor exclusively.

### Panola Mountain: Outdoor Recreation Demand

Panola Mountain State Park is not a well-known name outside Georgia, but within the metro outdoor recreation community it occupies a distinct position. As the flagship of the Arabia Mountain National Heritage Area — a federally designated landscape protecting granite monadnock formations — the park offers ecological experiences genuinely rare in the southeast. Visitors traveling to Stockbridge for the park are motivated by a specific destination, not geographic convenience. That specificity translates to deliberate trip planning and a preference for longer stays.

### The Relocation Demand Layer

Henry County's rapid residential growth has created a consistent demand segment often overlooked in STR analysis: families and individuals in transition. Corporate relocations, home purchases and sales, and new construction delays all create situations where quality short-term housing is needed for weeks or months. Stockbridge, as the county's northern anchor, absorbs a significant portion of this demand.

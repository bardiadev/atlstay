## Athens, GA: Managing Short-Term Rentals in the Classic City

Athens has one of the most recognizable identities of any city its size in the American South. It is a college town, but the University of Georgia's culture permeates far beyond the campus boundaries — the Bulldogs' football program is among the most followed in the country, the Athens music scene has produced national acts across multiple decades, and the city's food and arts culture has drawn recognition well beyond the state. For short-term rental owners, this translates to a market anchored by predictable demand spikes and a steady floor of year-round visitors.

### Football Season: The Market's Center of Gravity

Sanford Stadium seats over 90,000 people, and the Georgia Bulldogs regularly field nationally ranked teams. Seven home games per season — roughly from late August through November — create seven defined demand events for Athens vacation rentals, each with its own pricing opportunity. Rivalry games, Homecoming, and high-profile non-conference matchups pull in visitors from across Georgia and beyond, many of whom book months in advance once the schedule is released.

For property owners, football season is not just high demand — it is predictable high demand. Unlike weather-driven outdoor recreation markets or festivals that shift dates year to year, the UGA football schedule publishes in the spring and allows proactive management. Keystone Stays prices football weekends as individual events: each game gets a dedicated pricing assessment based on opponent, time of day, and historical demand patterns, not a blanket seasonal rate.

### Graduation, Families, and Year-Round UGA Demand

Spring graduation in May is Athens's second-largest demand event of the year. The ceremony draws families from across Georgia, many of whom want the space and privacy of a rented home rather than a hotel. Properties with 3–5 bedrooms and comfortable gathering spaces — for the family dinner after the ceremony, for the out-of-state relatives who flew in — are especially well-suited to graduation weekend demand.

The broader UGA academic calendar provides a structure of demand throughout the year: orientation weekends, Parents Weekend in the fall, move-in weekends in August, and the Spring Game in April all generate visitor traffic. A well-managed Athens property earns across this calendar, not just in October.

### The Music Scene

Athens has a legitimate claim to one of America's most storied music cultures. The 40 Watt Club, the Georgia Theatre, and the overall music ecosystem that produced R.E.M. and the B-52's continue to draw music tourists who are distinctly different from the football-weekend visitor profile. These guests tend to book shorter stays, arrive on a Thursday or Friday, and care more about walkability to downtown venues than proximity to Sanford Stadium. Managing an Athens property well means building listing copy that speaks to both audiences.

## Monroe's Short-Term Rental Market: Antiques District and Walton County Heritage

Monroe's STR market is defined by a specific kind of visitor: the guest who comes to Monroe for Monroe — for the antiques district, the historic square, or the small-town character that the city has preserved while much of the metro around it has been replaced by uniform development.

### The Antiques District

Monroe has built a reputation as a genuine antiques destination, with multiple dealers operating in and around the historic downtown. Collectors and buyers who travel specifically for antiques — estate furniture hunters, vintage retail buyers stocking inventory, couples browsing for home pieces — are a travel segment that plans ahead, often spends multiple days in a destination, and prefers the space of a rental home over a hotel room. Monroe's concentration of dealers makes it a practical overnight destination for serious buyers.

### Historic Courthouse Square

The Walton County Courthouse square preserves the civic and commercial architecture of a late-nineteenth-century Georgia county seat in unusually intact form. The courthouse itself, the surrounding commercial buildings, and the growing restaurant presence in historic storefronts give the square an authentic character that resonates with heritage travelers who seek genuine places over reproduced environments.

Guests who travel from Atlanta for a weekend away from the city's density — and who want real small-town character rather than a suburb — find Monroe's downtown to be a legitimate destination.

### County Seat Demand

As the Walton County seat, Monroe generates consistent civic, legal, and government visitor demand that adds a reliable weekday layer independent of leisure travel patterns.

## Dunwoody's Short-Term Rental Market: The Corporate Stability Advantage

Dunwoody incorporated as a city in 2008, carved out of unincorporated DeKalb County, and has consistently ranked among the most desirable residential communities in metro Atlanta. For short-term rental owners, Dunwoody's defining asset isn't its parks or its restaurants — it's its position at the center of one of the Southeast's most concentrated corporate employment zones.

### Perimeter Center: Built-In Weekday Demand

Perimeter Center is the name given to the dense office corridor straddling the I-285/GA-400 interchange, split between Dunwoody and Sandy Springs. Fortune 500 regional headquarters, consulting firms, healthcare systems, and technology companies maintain large operations here. The result is a near-continuous stream of business travelers — consultants, trainers, contractors, corporate transferees — who need furnished short-term rentals throughout the year.

For Dunwoody property owners, this means something that pure leisure markets can't offer: weekday demand that doesn't depend on weather, events, or season. A well-configured corporate rental in Dunwoody generates bookings on Tuesday nights in January, not just summer weekends.

The extended-stay segment is particularly valuable. A four-week booking from a Perimeter Center consultant fills an entire month of occupancy, requires far fewer turnover cleanings than nightly bookings, and typically produces less wear on the property than a high-turnover leisure calendar. Positioning for this segment — with home-office setup, quality internet, and a fully stocked kitchen — requires intentional listing design that ATLStay handles for every client.

### Dunwoody Village and the Weekend Leisure Layer

Dunwoody Village has developed into a genuine dining and gathering destination, anchored by restaurants and independently owned businesses that draw visitors from around the metro. Weekend leisure bookings from guests who want a northern Atlanta base while visiting family, exploring local dining, or attending events in the area supplement the weekday corporate calendar.

### MARTA and the Perimeter City Connection

The Dunwoody and Medical Center MARTA stations give the market a transit connection to the rest of Atlanta that many Perimeter suburbs lack. Business travelers who prefer not to rent a car can reach Dunwoody directly from the airport — a practical selling point for listings positioned toward the corporate segment.

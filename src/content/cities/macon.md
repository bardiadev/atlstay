## Macon: Georgia's Most Underrated Vacation Rental Market

Macon sits at the geographic center of Georgia, approximately 85 miles south of Atlanta along I-75 — close enough to serve as a day trip from the metro, far enough to feel distinctly its own place. It is a city with a remarkable cultural depth that its modest national profile does not reflect. For vacation rental owners, that gap between genuine demand and broad awareness creates opportunity.

### The World's Largest Cherry Blossom Festival

Macon has 350,000-plus Yoshino cherry trees — a concentration so extraordinary that the city was named the Cherry Blossom Capital of the World. The trees bloom for approximately two weeks in mid-March, and the International Cherry Blossom Festival — held since 1982 — has grown into the largest cherry blossom festival anywhere, drawing visitors from across the United States and internationally.

The festival is the single most significant demand event in the central Georgia STR calendar. Hotels book out weeks in advance. Private homes within the historic Intown neighborhoods offer the accommodation that festival visitors actually want: a porch to sit on, a kitchen for breakfast, a yard where the blossoms are visible from your own door. Pricing for festival week requires specific expertise — the demand ceiling is real and the competition is limited — and capturing it correctly can represent the equivalent of months of standard occupancy revenue.

### Music Pilgrimage and Cultural Tourism

Macon's music heritage is not a footnote — it is a genuine pilgrimage circuit. The Allman Brothers Band Museum at the Big House (the Vineville Avenue house where the band lived in 1969–1973) is a functioning museum that draws devoted visitors from around the world. The Otis Redding Foundation maintains the singer's legacy and hosts events. The Georgia Music Hall of Fame has roots here. Little Richard grew up in Macon and returned throughout his life.

For music-focused travelers — particularly Baby Boomers and Gen X visitors raised on Southern rock and soul — a Macon trip is intentional and emotionally resonant. These guests book multi-night stays, treat properties respectfully, and leave detailed reviews. They are exactly the audience that well-maintained historic Intown homes are built to serve.

### Mercer and the Institutional Anchor

Mercer University's presence ensures that demand is never entirely dependent on tourism events. The university's medical school, law school, and undergraduate programs generate family and professional travel year-round. Mercer's concert hall and performance venues draw regional audiences.

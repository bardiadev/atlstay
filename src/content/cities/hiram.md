## Hiram's Short-Term Rental Market: Paulding County's Commercial Core

Hiram functions differently from most west-metro Atlanta communities in one important way: it has commercial density. While neighboring communities are primarily residential, Hiram's position at the intersection of major Paulding County roads produced a concentration of retail, dining, services, and business activity that generates its own accommodation demand.

### Commercial Hub Demand

Businesses and service operations concentrated in Hiram — and the vendors, contractors, and corporate visitors those businesses attract — create a reliable demand layer that isn't seasonal and doesn't depend on a single attraction. A quality STR in Hiram can capture this commercial-visitor market more efficiently than properties in lower-activity communities nearby.

### Silver Comet Trail Access

The Silver Comet Trail passes through Paulding County, with access points that give Hiram visitors a practical entry to the trail. The trail extends east toward Smyrna and northwest toward the Alabama state line, offering everything from casual recreational rides to serious multi-day cycling expeditions. Guests staging a trail trip from the less-crowded west end find Hiram STRs a more practical option than properties at the Smyrna starting point.

### A Genuine Supply Gap

Paulding County's hotel infrastructure remains limited relative to its commercial activity and population. Quality STR accommodations that fill this gap — for business travelers, contractors on extended assignments, and families visiting local residents — face a favorable demand-to-supply ratio compared to closer-in Atlanta markets.

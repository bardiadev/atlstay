## Savannah's Short-Term Rental Market: Demand That Rarely Sleeps

Savannah occupies a rare position in the Georgia tourism landscape: it is simultaneously a historic preservation showpiece, a working arts city (home to one of the largest art and design schools in the country), a culinary destination, and the site of one of the most celebrated public festivals in the American South. That combination produces vacation rental demand that is broad, deep, and surprisingly consistent across seasons.

### What Drives Demand

The anchor event is St. Patrick's Day. Savannah's celebration draws hundreds of thousands of visitors over the extended weekend — filling every hotel room in the city and pushing short-term rental demand to its annual ceiling. Guests book months in advance. Properties within walking distance of River Street and Forsyth Park command the highest rates, but demand spreads across the entire Historic District and into adjacent neighborhoods.

Beyond March, Savannah's tourism infrastructure is built around year-round programming. The Savannah Food & Wine Festival and the Savannah Book Festival draw regional visitors in the fall. SCAD's twice-annual fashion shows bring an international design crowd. Ghost tours operate every night of the year and have become a genuine part of the city's identity. The Savannah College of Art and Design's graduation ceremonies fill weekends in the spring and fall, with families booking the kind of private accommodations that hotels can't provide.

Weddings are a structural demand driver that is easy to underestimate. Savannah is one of the top destination-wedding markets in the Southeast, and wedding parties — often eight to twenty guests — strongly prefer private homes over hotel blocks. Properties with outdoor space, multiple bedrooms, and kitchens are particularly well-suited to this segment.

### What Homes Perform

Historic row houses and carriage houses in the Landmark Historic District are the market's prestige inventory. Guests pay a premium for exposed brick, period details, and proximity to the squares. Garden-level units with private courtyards attract couples and honeymoon travelers who book well in advance and leave excellent reviews.

The Victorian District and Thomas Square Streetcar Historic District offer a slightly different profile: similar architectural character, more residential feel, lower acquisition cost, and a guest mix that skews toward travelers who prefer walkable-but-quieter over River Street energy.

### Regulatory Landscape

Savannah's short-term rental regulations have been an evolving area. The city has tightened some requirements in recent years while working to balance tourism revenue with neighborhood quality-of-life concerns. Properties in residential zoning districts typically require a Special Use Permit in addition to a Business Tax Certificate. Rules differ by historic district designation and zoning classification.

## McDonough's Short-Term Rental Market: Historic Square, Corridor Growth, and Event Demand

McDonough is doing something genuinely difficult: preserving an authentic historic character while absorbing rapid suburban growth. That balance creates a short-term rental market with two distinct demand profiles operating simultaneously.

### The Historic Square as a Destination

The 1897 Romanesque courthouse is the center of a square that has evolved into one of the I-75 south corridor's most compelling commercial districts. Locally owned restaurants, boutique shops, and year-round event programming give McDonough a downtown that visitors seek out rather than pass through.

The calendar of events matters for STR owners. The Geranium Festival in May is a significant regional event that fills available accommodation well in advance. The Heritage Festival in fall and Christmas on the Square in December create additional windows where demand exceeds supply and nightly rates reflect it. These peak windows, managed with proper dynamic pricing, produce a meaningful portion of annual revenue.

### The Growth Corridor Dynamic

I-75 made McDonough a viable commuter suburb, and corporate investment has followed population. Business travelers visiting the I-75 south corridor — from distribution center management to healthcare administration to construction project oversight — need furnished, short-term housing that neither chain hotels nor month-to-month leases serve efficiently. Quality STR properties fill this gap consistently.

### Film Production Presence

McDonough's picturesque square has attracted film and television production work, bringing entertainment-industry crews who need short-term housing for the duration of their engagement. Production stays book for weeks and generate lower turnover than leisure bookings — an efficient revenue segment for any Keystone-managed property.

## East Point's Short-Term Rental Market: Studio Demand and the World's Busiest Airport

East Point occupies a position that very few STR markets in Georgia can claim: immediate adjacency to the world's busiest airport and the presence of a major studio production campus within city limits.

### Tyler Perry Studios and the Production Economy

Tyler Perry Studios sits on the former Fort McPherson site — 330 acres of what was once a U.S. Army installation — and operates as one of the largest studio campuses in the country. Productions at Tyler Perry Studios bring large crews to East Point for extended periods. The production economy is different from leisure tourism: it books in advance, it pays for quality, and it doesn't need the city to be a destination in itself — it needs the housing to function well.

An East Point STR positioned for production crew stays can achieve multi-week bookings that create the kind of calendar stability that leisure-only properties rarely see.

### Hartsfield-Jackson: The Demand Floor

East Point's position within the ATL Airport District means there is a baseline demand for short-term accommodation that exists independently of events, seasons, or trends. Airline crews operate on fixed rotation schedules that generate layover accommodation needs 365 days a year. Business travelers transiting through ATL on multi-day visits need a base that's more comfortable and cost-effective than an airport hotel. Early-morning flight passengers consistently seek accommodation within 20-30 minutes of the terminal.

This demand floor doesn't produce the highest nightly rates, but it creates occupancy consistency that volatile leisure markets don't achieve.

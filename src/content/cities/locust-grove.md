## Locust Grove's Short-Term Rental Market: Outlets, Sanctuary, and I-75 Demand

Locust Grove occupies a specific kind of STR niche — a destination-anchored corridor stop on a major interstate. The market isn't defined by downtown character or a single event calendar; it's defined by layered, recurring visitor draws that produce demand across multiple guest types throughout the year.

### Tanger Outlets: A Group-Travel Generator

The Tanger Outlets Locust Grove complex is a genuine regional draw, pulling shoppers from Atlanta, Macon, and counties in between. Outlet shopping trips are frequently social — friend groups, family outings, couples' weekends — and those travelers often prefer a shared rental space over separate hotel rooms. A well-positioned property near the outlets, marketed clearly to this audience, can capture demand that a standard hotel can't easily serve.

### Noah's Ark and Family Visitor Demand

Noah's Ark Animal Sanctuary is a legitimate attraction, housing a large collection of rescued animals including big cats, bears, and primates. It draws visitors who make a specific trip for the experience — often families with children who are staying overnight and want space for kids to decompress after a day out. This is a demand driver that most nearby communities don't share.

### Interstate Position and Extended Stays

I-75 south of Atlanta is not just a commuter corridor — it's a route that connects Atlanta to Middle Georgia, South Georgia, and Florida. Travelers who prefer a quality mid-trip stop over another chain hotel room represent a consistent guest profile. Longer-term contractors working in Henry County's ongoing residential development add another weekday layer.

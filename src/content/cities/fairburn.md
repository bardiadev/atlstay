## Fairburn's Short-Term Rental Market: Renaissance Festival, Historic Character, and Film Industry Demand

Fairburn holds a STR positioning that no other south Fulton community can replicate: an authentic historic downtown, a nationally recognized recurring festival event, and a growing film industry presence — all within reach of Hartsfield-Jackson Airport's demand gravity.

### Georgia Renaissance Festival: A Spring Calendar Anchor

The Georgia Renaissance Festival runs for seven spring weekends at its permanent site in Fairburn and is one of the largest and most elaborate Renaissance events in the southeastern US. The festival has loyal multi-year attendees, draws visitors from neighboring states, and generates the kind of committed festival tourism that produces real lodging demand. Guests who want to attend multiple weekends or who travel from out of state specifically for the festival will book a local STR before they'll drive from Atlanta each day.

For a Fairburn property owner, these festival weekends represent predictable demand spikes that a dynamic pricing strategy can convert into meaningfully above-average rate performance.

### Historic Downtown Fairburn

Fairburn's downtown district preserves Victorian and early-twentieth-century commercial architecture in a form that most south-metro communities have long since lost to redevelopment. The historic depot, preserved streetscape, and growing restaurant and arts presence give the downtown an authentic character that resonates with the heritage-travel segment growing within domestic tourism.

### Film Production Growth

South Fulton County sits within the active perimeter of Georgia's screen production industry. Production crews on location in this zone represent a high-value extended-stay segment — longer bookings, professional guests, and rate tolerance that reflects the film industry's accommodation budgets.

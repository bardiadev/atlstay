## Morrow's Short-Term Rental Market: University, Archives, and Airport Demand

Morrow doesn't appear on most lists of Atlanta-area STR markets, but that oversight reflects how most management platforms approach market selection — by population density rather than demand structure. Morrow's demand structure is genuinely diverse.

### Clayton State University: The Anchor Institution

Clayton State University, located on a 214-acre campus in Morrow, brings consistent visitor cycles to the city that any nearby STR can benefit from. Graduation ceremonies, orientation weekends, athletic events, and academic conferences all generate predictable demand spikes. University families traveling for these occasions — parents, siblings, extended relatives — often prefer a comfortable home to a chain hotel, especially for multi-night stays where cooking access matters.

### The Georgia Archives: A Niche Demand Driver

The Georgia Archives is one of the Southeast's most significant genealogical and historical research facilities. Researchers who travel to Morrow to access archival records often need two, three, or four nights to complete their work. This segment — genealogists, historians, attorneys researching property chains, academics — is fundamentally different from leisure travelers: they book further in advance, care about workspace and quiet, and have no interest in nightlife proximity. A well-configured Morrow STR that caters to this segment can build a remarkably consistent midweek calendar.

### Airport Proximity and the Coming BRT Connection

At 12 miles from Hartsfield-Jackson, Morrow already captures airport-adjacent demand. MARTA's planned Rapid Southlake Bus Rapid Transit route, which will connect the airport through Clayton County to Southlake Mall, will deepen that connectivity further once operational.

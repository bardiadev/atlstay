## Chamblee's Short-Term Rental Market: Buford Highway, MARTA, and Global Culture

Chamblee has undergone a quiet transformation over the past decade that has made it one of metro Atlanta's most interesting addresses for short-term rental investment. What was once a mid-century suburb near the Peachtree DeKalb Airport has evolved into a destination — one anchored by the Buford Highway food corridor, a revitalized antiques and restaurant downtown, and a MARTA connection that genuinely matters to international travelers.

### Buford Highway: The World in One Corridor

Southern Living and food publications nationwide have cited Buford Highway as one of the most important culinary destinations in the American South. Vietnamese pho shops, Korean BBQ restaurants, Chinese dumpling houses, Colombian bakeries, and Filipino grocery stores line the stretch running through Chamblee — producing a density of international cuisine that is almost unparalleled outside of major gateway cities. This drives a specific type of traveler: the food-motivated visitor who plans an entire weekend around eating their way through the corridor. For a Chamblee STR host, these guests are ideal — they are engaged, appreciative, and often return.

### MARTA and the International Guest

The Chamblee station makes this city genuinely accessible to guests who land at Hartsfield-Jackson and don't want to deal with Atlanta's traffic or a rental car. That includes international visitors, conference attendees, and repeat business travelers who have learned to use MARTA from the airport. Listing near the station with clear transit instructions creates a meaningful competitive advantage.

### Downtown Chamblee's Emerging Scene

The downtown around Chamblee's MARTA station has developed its own restaurant row and weekend energy. Combined with the antiques district — one of the largest in the Southeast — it gives the city a leisure identity beyond the highway corridor.

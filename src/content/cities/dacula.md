## Dacula's Short-Term Rental Market: Sports Travel, School Zones, and Eastern Gwinnett Growth

Dacula sits at an intersection that matters for STR demand: east Gwinnett's fastest-growing residential corridor meets one of metro Atlanta's most active zones for youth sports activity. The result is a market with dependable, family-oriented demand across three distinct booking drivers.

### Youth Sports Tourism: Dacula's Weekend Demand Engine

Youth sports tourism is one of the most underappreciated STR demand segments in suburban Atlanta. East Gwinnett — including Dacula and the surrounding area — has significant tournament infrastructure for baseball, soccer, football, and other sports. Families driving to Dacula from neighboring counties or from across the Southeast for weekend tournaments are a natural STR guest: they need space for four to six people, they arrive Friday afternoon, and they leave Sunday. They have almost no flexibility in their destination — the tournament venue determines where they stay — and they consistently prefer a private home to crowded hotel corridors with limited parking.

Active management around the tournament calendar is one of the clearest pricing levers for Dacula owners.

### School Zone Reputation and Relocation Demand

Mill Creek and Dacula high school attendance zones are among the most sought-after in Gwinnett County, which means families actively relocate to the area for school access. Relocation buyers often close on a new home several weeks before their previous residence is available, or arrive in the area months early to get settled. This creates demand for longer-stay rentals — two weeks to two months — that are best served by private residential properties rather than extended-stay hotels.

### The UGA Corridor

Hwy 316 connects Dacula eastward to Athens and the University of Georgia campus in about 45 minutes. During UGA football season, families who want more space than Athens hotels provide — and don't want to pay Athens game-weekend hotel rates — regularly base in east Gwinnett. Dacula is well-positioned for this overflow demand.

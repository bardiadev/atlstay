## Suwanee's Short-Term Rental Market: Premium Suburban Demand and Town Center Appeal

Suwanee has spent two decades positioning itself as Gwinnett's premier residential destination, and that positioning translates directly into STR economics. Guests arrive with an assumption of quality — well-maintained homes, clean neighborhoods, good access to dining — and properties that confirm that assumption consistently earn the reviews and repeat visits that drive long-term revenue.

### Town Center Park and the Walkable Downtown Effect

Suwanee Town Center Park is the anchor of one of suburban Atlanta's most successful urban-park concepts. The award-winning park sits at the center of a walkable district that includes independent restaurants, boutique retail, and an amphitheater that hosts concerts, outdoor movies, seasonal festivals, and weekly community events throughout the year.

For STR owners, Town Center creates demand on its own terms — guests who specifically seek out Suwanee for access to the downtown experience — while also elevating the appeal of the broader market. Properties within walking distance of Town Center command a measurable premium; those within a short drive still benefit from the quality signal the area projects.

### The Corporate Satellite Boulevard Corridor

The Satellite Boulevard corridor through Suwanee and adjacent Duluth hosts a meaningful concentration of corporate and technology employment. Business travelers on extended visits — consulting engagements, project teams, relocating employees in temporary housing — generate weekday demand that the leisure market doesn't. A well-positioned Suwanee property can serve a corporate guest on a two-week engagement just as effectively as a family arriving for the weekend.

### An Affluent Market That Rewards Premium Properties

Suwanee's demographics are a meaningful STR advantage. Households in the area have among the highest median incomes in Gwinnett County, which means visiting family, friends, and colleagues arrive with expectations that match. Properties that invest in premium amenities — high-speed internet, quality linens, a well-equipped kitchen, thoughtful staging — earn disproportionately better reviews and substantially higher average daily rates in this market than in more value-oriented suburbs.

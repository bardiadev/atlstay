## Buford's Short-Term Rental Market: Three Demand Drivers, One Market

Buford is one of the few Gwinnett cities where a short-term rental owner can honestly argue the property will be busy in every season. Mall of Georgia draws year-round retail and dining visitors. Lake Lanier drives spring and summer water-recreation demand. Coolray Field fills the baseball calendar from April through September. That's a demand profile most suburban markets would need three cities to replicate.

### Mall of Georgia: A Regional Draw That Fills Weekends

Mall of Georgia at 3333 Buford Drive is one of the largest shopping malls in the Southeast. It anchors a retail and dining corridor that includes lifestyle centers, restaurants, and entertainment venues that draw visitors not just from the Atlanta metro but from across northeast Georgia and beyond. Out-of-town shoppers and mall-visit groups — particularly during peak retail seasons, Black Friday through New Year's, and back-to-school — are consistent Airbnb guests who prefer a private home to paying hotel rates near the mall.

### Lake Lanier: Spring and Summer Water-Recreation Demand

Lake Lanier sits immediately north of Buford. Several lake access points, marinas, and recreation areas are within a short drive of downtown Buford, making the city one of the most convenient residential bases for lake recreation in the metro. Spring and summer — particularly Memorial Day through Labor Day — drive this demand segment hard. Families who want to spend a week on the lake consistently choose private home rentals over hotels, especially when they're traveling with boats, gear, and kids.

### Coolray Field and the Baseball Season Calendar

Coolray Field, home of the Gwinnett Stripers (Triple-A affiliate of the Atlanta Braves), runs a full home schedule from April through September. Weekend games, theme nights, and Braves prospect promotion events reliably draw fans and families. Baseball-season demand adds a steady event overlay to Buford's leisure and retail base.

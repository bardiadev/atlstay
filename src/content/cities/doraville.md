## Doraville's Short-Term Rental Market: Film Industry, Transit Access, and a City Reinventing Itself

Doraville is arguably the most dynamically changing city in DeKalb County right now. The redevelopment of the former GM Doraville Assembly plant into Assembly Atlanta — a 127-acre film and entertainment campus with 19 sound stages — has transformed the city's demand profile in a way that most suburban STR markets never experience.

### Assembly Atlanta: Film Crew Housing Demand

Georgia's entertainment industry has grown into the third-largest film production market in the world, powered by the state's competitive tax credit. Assembly Atlanta has positioned Doraville at the center of this industry, with multi-month productions cycling through on a regular basis. Film and TV productions have specific housing needs: they bring in large crews from out of state who need short-term accommodation near the studio for weeks or months at a time. Unlike leisure travelers who book impulsively, production companies often book housing blocks in advance, with consistent minimum stay requirements that reduce turnover burden for hosts.

### The MARTA Terminus Advantage

Doraville station is the northern terminus of the MARTA Gold Line, providing direct rail service into downtown Atlanta and all the way to Hartsfield-Jackson. For guests who arrive without a car — including international travelers and those attending Atlanta conferences — Doraville's MARTA access makes it a genuinely useful base. Properties close to the station can compete for transit-oriented guests who might otherwise default to Buckhead or Midtown.

### Buford Highway and Neighborhood Character

The Buford Highway corridor extends through Doraville, offering some of the best Vietnamese, Latin American, and Korean cuisine in the metro. The city's ongoing redevelopment — including new mixed-use projects and a redesigned town green — is building an urban identity that will sustain STR demand well beyond the film-industry catalyst.

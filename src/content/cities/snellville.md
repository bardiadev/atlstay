## Snellville's Short-Term Rental Market: A Community-Driven Demand Profile

Snellville has invested meaningfully in building a downtown identity around The Grove at Towne Center, and that investment pays dividends for short-term rental owners. Unlike most Gwinnett suburbs, which depend entirely on Atlanta-area mega-venues for event demand, Snellville generates its own event calendar — one that runs year-round and is deeply embedded in the local community.

### The Grove at Towne Center: Snellville's Event Engine

The Grove at Towne Center is the largest project Snellville has ever undertaken and the defining feature of its revitalized downtown. The venue hosts the Downtown Tunes Concert Series, outdoor movies, Snellville Days (one of Gwinnett's biggest annual festivals), holiday celebrations, and an ongoing calendar of community gatherings.

For STR owners, The Grove calendar provides consistent event-demand signals that make dynamic pricing significantly more effective. An outdoor concert series on a Friday evening in the spring pushes local demand in ways that flat-rate pricing never captures.

### The Gwinnett Environmental and Heritage Center

The Gwinnett Environmental and Heritage Center adds a dimension of educational and heritage tourism to the market. The facility draws school groups, event attendees, and visitors with an interest in the environmental and cultural heritage of the Gwinnett region — a segment that often books midweek and travels in small groups, adding occupancy outside the leisure weekend peak.

### A Dense Residential Market with Extended-Stay Demand

Snellville's residential density and strong school zones create demand from families in transition — relocating households, corporate arrivals, and extended-stay guests who need months, not nights. Properties configured for longer stays (full kitchen, dedicated workspace, laundry access) perform well here.

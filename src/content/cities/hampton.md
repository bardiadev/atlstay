## Hampton's Short-Term Rental Market: Event-Driven Demand and the Speedway Effect

Hampton, Georgia, gives STR owners access to something most suburban markets simply don't have: a world-class motorsports venue that generates predictable, high-intensity demand on a published calendar.

### Atlanta Motor Speedway: The Core Demand Driver

EchoPark Speedway — the operating name for Atlanta Motor Speedway — is one of NASCAR's historic tracks, known for high-speed racing that has made it a fan favorite for decades. The track's recent redevelopment brought new layouts and expanded seating, and NASCAR events here consistently attract large national audiences beyond just the local fan base.

For STR owners, this matters in a specific way: race weekend demand is calendared, predictable, and extremely price-inelastic. Fans plan trips months in advance. Corporate and media guests need accommodation that's confirmed well before event week. The supply of quality accommodation close to the Speedway has historically been thin relative to this demand, which means well-managed properties capture premium rates that bear little relationship to the market's normal baseline pricing.

### Beyond Racing: A Year-Round Event Venue

The Speedway's event calendar extends well beyond NASCAR weekends. The Georgia State Fair, the Atlanta Air Show, the Motorama car show, and other major events all create demand windows that a Hampton STR can capture. Taken together, these events represent multiple high-rate weeks per year on top of the racing calendar.

### The Hampton Baseline

Between events, Hampton functions as a quiet Henry County suburb with a rural character that appeals to guests seeking space and quiet within the metro footprint. Properties with outdoor space, fire pits, and clear sky views book well for that appeal — and the event premium on top more than justifies premium property investment.

## Kennesaw's Short-Term Rental Market: Battlefield Heritage, University Events, and Corridor Business Travel

Kennesaw gets overlooked in conversations about Atlanta's STR market, partly because it lacks the brand recognition of Marietta or the tech cachet of Alpharetta. But for owners who understand the demand drivers, Kennesaw offers a genuinely year-round booking profile built on three distinct pillars.

### Kennesaw Mountain: The Heritage Tourism Foundation

Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park is one of the most visited units in the National Park Service in the Southeast. The mountain itself is one of the most prominent landmarks in Cobb County, offering hiking trails and a Civil War history that draws visitors from across the country. The Civil War attractions around the park — including the Southern Museum of Civil War and Locomotive History in adjacent Kennesaw city — extend the heritage tourism draw.

Heritage visitors often plan multi-day itineraries centered on Civil War sites across north Georgia, and Kennesaw sits at a natural stopping point. A property positioned around easy access to the mountain and the museum captures a guest segment with strong demand throughout the spring, fall, and summer.

### Kennesaw State University: The Academic Demand Calendar

KSU has grown into one of the largest universities in Georgia, bringing with it a robust annual calendar of events that generate predictable hotel and STR demand. Graduation weekend alone pulls thousands of families who need overnight accommodations. Family Weekend, KSU Owls football at Fifth Third Bank Stadium, homecoming, and the steady flow of visiting families throughout the academic year all create demand that concentrations well above average nightly rates.

Parents of students often become repeat guests — booking multiple times per academic year for visits, move-in and move-out weekends, and special events. Cultivating these return guests through a well-managed listing is one of the compounding advantages of active management in a university market.

### The I-75 Business Corridor

Barrett Parkway and the Town Center area along I-75 house a significant corporate and retail presence that generates weekday business travel. Professionals working on projects in northwest Atlanta, transferees settling into new corporate roles, and contractors on extended assignments all look for furnished short-term rentals in the Kennesaw corridor.

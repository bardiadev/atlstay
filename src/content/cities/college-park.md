## College Park's Short-Term Rental Market: MARTA, Airport, and Film Demand

College Park's geography is a specific kind of STR advantage: not a scenic destination, but a supremely functional one for a demanding guest base that values access over atmosphere.

### The MARTA Advantage

College Park is one of the few Atlanta-area STR markets where a guest can realistically arrive at Hartsfield-Jackson, take MARTA two stops, and walk to their accommodation without a rental car. For international travelers, business visitors, and transit-oriented guests, that eliminates the most friction-filled element of travel logistics. MARTA's direct airport connection means College Park attracts guests who have made a conscious decision to prioritize transit access — a segment that tends to be reliable, repeat-booking, and less concerned with getting the absolute lowest price.

### Airport Proximity as a Business Proposition

Five minutes from the terminal, College Park properties sit at the core of the ATL Airport District's accommodation demand. Airline crew layovers — on fixed rotation schedules that operate regardless of season, events, or market trends — create the most reliable demand baseline in the metro. Early-morning flight passengers, connecting travelers on multi-day visits, and corporate visitors whose Atlanta meetings are airport-area rather than downtown all feed this baseline consistently.

### Historic College Park and the Emerging Destination Layer

Historic College Park's Main Street is undergoing the revitalization pattern visible in similar corridors around the metro — independent restaurants, boutique retail, and community investment in the built environment. Productions that need authentic neighborhood character within Atlanta's film-friendly ecosystem have found College Park, creating an entertainment-industry demand layer that sits on top of the transit and airport baseline.

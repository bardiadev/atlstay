## Winder's Short-Term Rental Market: Fort Yargo State Park and Barrow County

Winder's STR market is built on two distinct demand foundations: a world-class state park that drives year-round outdoor recreation visits, and a county seat function that generates consistent business and civic accommodation demand independent of any leisure calendar.

### Fort Yargo State Park: Year-Round Recreation Demand

Fort Yargo State Park is among Georgia's most heavily visited state parks, and it's easy to understand why. The park's centerpiece is Lake Yargo, with a swimming beach, rental kayaks and paddleboards, and fishing access. An extensive mountain bike trail network has become a destination draw for the cycling community across the northeast metro. Tent and RV camping serves traditional outdoor visitors, while group facilities draw family reunions and organized outings.

The guest segment that makes Fort Yargo particularly valuable for STR demand is visitors who want park access without the limitations of camping. Families with young children who want lake swimming but a real kitchen for meals. Mountain bikers who want to arrive with bikes and gear and have a place to clean up properly after a long trail day. This guest profile actively seeks comfortable accommodation close to the park, and quality STR inventory in Winder fills that need directly.

### Barrow County's Growth Trajectory

Barrow County has maintained above-average growth for over a decade, driven by its position on the Hwy 316 corridor and the relative affordability it offers compared to Gwinnett and other closer-in metro counties. That growth generates contractor and corporate accommodation demand on a sustained basis — workers who are on multi-week assignments in the county and need something better than a roadside extended-stay hotel.

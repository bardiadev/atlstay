## Lake Oconee: Georgia's Premium Lake Market

Lake Oconee is a 19,000-acre reservoir on the Oconee River in Putnam and Greene counties — two hours east of Atlanta, tucked into the rolling Georgia Piedmont where pine-and-hardwood shoreline meets clear-water recreational lake. On its own, that description fits several Georgia reservoirs. What distinguishes Lake Oconee is what was built on its shore.

### Reynolds Lake Oconee and the Premium Floor

Reynolds Lake Oconee is a 12,000-acre master-planned community that surrounds much of the lake's western shore. Its development includes six championship golf courses — including Great Waters, designed by Jack Nicklaus, and The National, considered among the finest golf venues in Georgia. The Forbes Five-Star Ritz-Carlton Lodge, Reynolds Lake Oconee, provides a resort anchor that sets the expectations and the price benchmarks for the entire lake market.

That resort presence matters directly to STR owners because the Ritz-Carlton cannot accommodate every guest who wants to be at Lake Oconee. Corporate retreat groups, wedding parties using Reynolds event venues, multi-generational family vacations that need five bedrooms and a dock — these guests look beyond resort accommodations, and well-positioned private rentals capture them. The Ritz-Carlton effect is real: it establishes what premium Lake Oconee hospitality looks like, and private homes that deliver at that quality level can price accordingly.

### Golf Drives the Calendar

Lake Oconee's event and seasonal rhythm is heavily shaped by golf. Reynolds hosts member-guest tournaments, corporate invitational events, and charity golf outings that fill the community's calendar in spring and fall. Guests who travel to play Reynolds or Great Waters — and who are sophisticated enough travelers to want a private lakefront home rather than a resort room — represent a high-value demand segment that rewards quality listing content and premium amenities.

Golf-season weekends in April, May, September, and October require specific pricing strategies that recognize the elevated demand without alienating the recreational lake audience that fills the summer months. Active management captures both.

### Atlanta's Premium Lake Escape

For Atlanta's corporate and professional class, Lake Oconee functions as the prestige weekend destination — the lake you go to when you want something more refined than Lake Lanier's party-boat summer energy. The two-hour drive from Buckhead or Alpharetta is a manageable Friday evening trip. Executive retreat groups, board-level corporate outings, and high-net-worth family vacations all follow this pattern.

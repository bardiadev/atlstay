## Lilburn's Short-Term Rental Market: Old Town Character and Outdoor Appeal

Lilburn is one of Gwinnett's more distinctive markets for short-term rentals — a city that has preserved a genuine small-town character through its Old Town district while sitting squarely within reach of some of the metro's most popular outdoor attractions.

### Old Town Lilburn and the Walkable Weekend Experience

Originally developed as a railroad community in the late 1800s, Old Town Lilburn has evolved into one of Gwinnett's most appealing walkable neighborhoods. Local restaurants, boutique shops, the Lilburn City Park amphitheater, and a calendar of community events — concerts, art walks, farmers markets, holiday festivals — give the area a character that typical Gwinnett suburban corridors don't offer.

For STR guests, Old Town Lilburn represents something increasingly valuable in Atlanta's suburbs: a destination that doesn't require driving everywhere. Guests who arrive for a Stone Mountain trip or a weekend getaway and can walk to dinner are consistently more satisfied — and more likely to leave the kind of reviews that sustain long-term listing performance.

### Stone Mountain and Outdoor Recreation Demand

Stone Mountain Park, approximately 20 minutes east of Old Town Lilburn, is one of Georgia's top-attended attractions. The park's summer laser show series, hiking trails, and fall festivals draw consistent visitor volume, and Lilburn's residential neighborhoods provide convenient, comfortable alternatives to the limited hotel supply near the park.

The Wolf Creek Trail and Lilburn City Park add local outdoor appeal — particularly for families with children who want easy access to green space during their stay. Properties with yards, covered outdoor seating, and space for larger groups consistently outperform on this leisure segment.

### A Reliable Suburban Family Market

Lilburn's housing stock — predominantly well-maintained single-family homes with garage parking and outdoor space — is exactly the type of property the family and small-group STR guest prefers. This is not a market that depends on any single event or venue; it is driven by the sustained preference for private, residential-scale accommodation when visiting the eastern suburbs of Atlanta.
